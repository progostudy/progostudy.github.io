13. In this kind of interview the researcher ask a standard set of questions and nothing more.​


Structured interview is where the interviewer has prepared a set of questions.

In the context of interview questions, there are two types: unstructured and structured. Unstructured interview means the interviewer does not previously prepare questions to ask to the interviewee, and rather let the flow of the intervview process be, depending on the answer of the questions asked during the interview.

Meanwhile, on the contrary, structured interview means that the interview is simply determined in the case of what questions would be asked by the interviewer during the interview process. There can't be some additional questions since it would disrupt the set questions made prior to the interview process.
