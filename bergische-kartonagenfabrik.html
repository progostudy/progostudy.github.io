Answer:

240 ways

Step-by-step explanation:

Assuming "AD seat" means a specific seat labeled as "AD" and "li" means another specific seat labeled as "li", the problem can be solved as follows:

If 2 of the 6 students insist on sitting next to each other, we can treat them as a single unit. This reduces the problem to arranging 5 units (4 individual students and 1 pair) in the 5 remaining seats.

There are 5 ways to choose the seat for the pair (either AD or li), and 2 ways to arrange the pair within their chosen seat. Then there are 4 ways to choose a seat for the first remaining student, 3 ways to choose a seat for the second remaining student, 2 ways to choose a seat for the third remaining student, and 1 way to seat the last remaining student.

Therefore, the total number of ways to seat the 6 students is:

5 × 2 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 240

So there are 240 ways to seat the 6 students with the conditions given.
