7. Three patients in an intensive care unit are examined by the resident doctor. one patient has brain damage from a stroke, another had a heart attack that severely damaged his heart muscle, and the third has severely damaged liver (a gland) from a crushing injury in a car accident. all three patients have stabilized and will survive, but only one will have full functional recovery through regeneration. which one and why


Answer:

The Third One

Explanation:

The liver is a unique organ. It is the only organ in the body that is able to regenerate that is completely repairing the damage. With most organs, such as the heart, the damaged tissue is replaced with a scar, like on the skin. The liver, however, is able to replace damaged tissue with new cells. The long-term complications of liver disease occur when regeneration is either incomplete or prevented by the progressive development of scar tissue within the liver. This occurs when the damaging agent such as a virus, a drug, alcohol, etc., continues to attack the liver and prevents complete regeneration. Once scar tissue has developed it is very difficult to reverse that process. Severe scarring of the liver is a condition known as cirrhosis. The development of cirrhosis indicates late-stage liver disease and is usually followed by the onset of complications. However, if no other complications arise, the patient’s liver will repair completely, and a liver biopsy after 30 days will appear completely normal with no signs of damage and no scar.

In contrast to the regeneration scenario; the patient with the severely damaged liver has a higher chance of healing through the process of regeneration.

27. The part of the brain that gives birth to new brain cells until adulthood.


Hippocampus is the part of the brain that gives birth to new brain cells until adulthood.

Hippocampus is a small, curved formation located in the medial temporal lobe of the brain. It is part of the limbic system and is very important because hippocampus' role is the formation of new memories that is also associated with emotions and learning. Its function is about the formation, organization and storage of new memories. It helps make short term memories to long term ones during sleep. Also, it helps connect certain emotions and sensations in these memories.

Hippocampus gives new brain cells, neurons, since birth and slowly declines through adulthood. New neurons are important because according to various  researches, hippocampus has been linked to improved memory and learning, and possibly even mood regulation. Since hippocampus plays an important role, if it is damaged, it will leave a large damage impact in certain types of memory such as amnesia or Alzheimer's disease. Also, age affects the function of the said organ because according to research, human hippocampus shrinks as time goes by.
