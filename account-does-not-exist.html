Answer:

C. Standard bank confirmation

The auditor typically tests for the existence of cash by using the standard bank confirmation procedure. This involves sending a written request to the client's bank to confirm the cash balances held in the client's name as of a specific date. The bank is asked to reply directly to the auditor, confirming the balance and providing any other relevant information, such as the existence of any liens or restrictions on the account.
