To answer the questions above, let's learn about the conditional sentence!

Zero Conditional Sentences

These sentences express one thing always causes another. Zero conditionals are used to express factual conditionals, but they are not factual statements. This sentence uses the "if" or " when" clause in the simple present tense in the front of a sentence.

The Structure of Zero Conditional

Present simple + present simple

Example:

If you cry, I don't want to buy ice cream for you

I feel happy when my boyfriend gives me an ice cream

First Conditional Sentences

These sentences express a result that is not certain, but likely to happen. These sentences use if and will + verb.

The Structure of First Conditional

if + Present simple + Subject + Will/Won't + Verb

For example:

If I cancel the trip, I will go to school tomorrow

If Sasha pays the electricity bill, she won't sleep over at my house

Second Conditional Sentences

Second conditional sentences are used to express something that is unlikely will be happened. We can use these sentences to talk about something unreal or impossible situation now or in the future.

The Structure of Second Conditional

if + past simple + would + verb base

For example:

If I were her, I would travel the world

If She was a billionaire, she would take you down

Third Conditional Sentences

Third conditional sentences are used to express situations that are impossible because they have already happened and cannot be changed. The third conditional sentence also can be used when we want to imagine a different past.

Structure of Third Conditional

If + past perfect + Subject + would have + past participle

For example:

If you had eaten, you would have had sick

If Rachel had studied, she would have failed
