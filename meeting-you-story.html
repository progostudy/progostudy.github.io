1. The story took place in Japan.

2. The characters in the story include Makato, the King, the people of his village, and the merchant.

3. Makato is described as a poor but kind-hearted boy who loves to play in the water, and loves to collect shells of different colors and shapes.

4. Makato's dream is to go to the capital of Japan, Sokhothai, to make his fortune.

5. Makato wanted to go to Sokhothai because he believed that if he had a larger amount of money, he would be able to help his family and his village.

6. Makato meets the King when he is thrown into the palace by the king's guards after finding a precious cowrie shell.

7. Makato takes the shell given to him by the King and uses it to purchase items he needs for his journey to Sokhothai.

8. If I were Makato, I would use the shell to make a remembrance of his wonderful journey, so that it may remind him of what he had achieved and how far he had come.

9. Yes, Makato has reached his dream in the story. Despite the difficulties he faced, he still persevered in achieving his dream of going to Sokhothai.

10. The lessons we learn from this story include the importance of perseverence and hard work, and the idea that no matter where you come from, you can still achieve your dreams with enough courage and determination.
