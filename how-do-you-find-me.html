11. How do you find DO in F major scale? please help me here, thank youuu! < 3​


Answer:

;)

First of all a half step (also called a semitone) is the shortest distance between two keys on your piano. It’s the shortest distance in Western music. For instance C to C sharp is a half step. E to F is a half step. Two half steps make up a whole step (or whole tone). For instance, C to D is a whole step, and F to G is a whole step as well. A whole step means that you should skip a key. A half step means that you should play the next key.Using the formula, W-W-H-W-W-W-H, when we start on F, we move one whole step to G, one whole step to A, a half step to Bb, a whole step to C, a whole step to D, a whole step to E and a half step to F. And there we have it… the F major scale.The relative minor of F major is D minor. Both scales make use of the same notes. The difference is that for F major, F is the first note, while for D minor, D is the first note. The notes of the D minor scale are D – E – F – G – A – Bb – C – D.

15. How do you find (or compute) the width of the rectangle?(Can you give me an example?)​


Answer:

Area = lenght x width

Area ÷ lenght = width

Width = Area ÷ lenght

Step-by-step explanation:

Example: if an area of a rectangle is 100m^2 with a lenght of 25m what is the width of the rectangle?

Area = lenght x width

Width = Area ÷ lenght

Width = 100 ÷ 25

Width = 4

Therefore the Width of the rectangle is 4m
