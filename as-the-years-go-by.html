4. Alex and Joseph are students at the same school. If Alex has 6 more years of going to school in the same school than Joseph has, and the two of them have 16 years of going to the same school combined, how many years of going to school does Joseph have?


Answer:

Let's use J to represent the number of years Joseph has been going to school in the same school.

According to the problem, Alex has 6 more years of going to school in the same school than Joseph. So, Alex has J + 6 years of going to school in the same school.

The problem also states that the combined years of going to school for both Alex and Joseph is 16. So we can write an equation based on this information:

J + (J + 6) = 16

Simplifying the equation:

2J + 6 = 16

Subtracting 6 from both sides:

2J = 10

Dividing both sides by 2:

J = 5

Therefore, Joseph has been going to school in the same school for 5 years.
