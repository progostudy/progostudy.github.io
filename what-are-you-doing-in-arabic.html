25. why do you think the three arab hammock religions are unique you they're in their own way​


Answer:

When most people think of Abrahamic religions, they think of Judaism, Christianity, and Islam. There are more Abrahamic religions, such as the Baha'i Faith, Yezidi, Druze, Samaritan, and Rastafari, but this article will concentrate on the three most common. The term 'Abrahamic' refers to the central significance that the figure of Abraham plays in each of these cultures. Jews, Christians, and Muslims all turn to their sacred scriptures to learn about Abraham's past and how it has been interpreted over time. The Hebrew Bible, which includes the Torah (the first five books, or Pentateuch), the Prophets (Nevi'im), and the Writings, is the central text for Jews (Ketuvim). Genesis, the first book of the Torah, tells the narrative of Abraham.

Abraham is mentioned numerous times in both the Hebrew Bible and post-biblical rabbinical literature interpreting the biblical story (Midrash). For Christians, the Hebrew Bible is the Old Testament, which is the antecedent to the New Testament and tells the story of Jesus Christ's birth, ministry, death, and Resurrection, as well as the lives and preaching of his early followers. The Letters of St Paul are especially important for Christian interpretations of Abraham. In their holy book, the Qur'an, as well as in the Hadith, a collection of writings that transmit the Prophet Muhammad's words and actions, Muslims deal with the figure of Abraham/Ibrhm.
