29. Love God and your honor over all things; God, as the source of all truth, all justice, and all activity; your honor, the only power that obliges you to be truthful, just and industrious.WHAT DOES IT MEAN??? EXPLAIN IT ​


The statement "Love God and your honor over all things; God, as the source of all truth, all justice, and all activity; your honor, the only power that obliges you to be truthful, just and industrious" is an exhortation to prioritize one's relationship with God and one's personal integrity above all other things.

The statement suggests that God is the ultimate source of truth, justice, and activity, and therefore, one's devotion to God should guide one's actions and decisions. It also emphasizes the importance of maintaining one's honor, which is seen as the only force that obliges one to be truthful, just, and hardworking.

In essence, the statement is a call to live a life of integrity and to prioritize one's spiritual and moral values over material possessions or worldly success. It suggests that by living a life guided by these principles, one can achieve a sense of purpose and fulfillment that goes beyond mere material gain.


30. reflection of "for all have sinned and fall the glory of God"​


We all have sinned and we failed to reach the God's standard of glory. we failed to remain our purity of doing things. We all fall short of His "glory" because of our sin. God's glory, or the glory of Himself and His kingdom, is what He shares with those who are in His family, His children. Our sin, though, keeps us from sharing in His glory.
