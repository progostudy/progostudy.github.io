13. amidst progress in science, why is gothic literature still popular. 200 words​


Gothic literature has remained popular despite advances in science because it appeals to fundamental human emotions and desires. Gothic literature often explores the darker aspects of human nature, such as fear, death, and the supernatural, and it taps into our innate fascination with the unknown and the macabre. The gothic genre creates a sense of suspense and terror that continues to captivate audiences, even in an age where science has provided answers to many of life's mysteries.

Furthermore, gothic literature often deals with themes of power, inequality, and morality, which are still relevant today. The genre's ability to delve into these themes in a fantastical and imaginative manner makes it appealing to readers who are looking to escape from the realities of the world and experience something new and different.

Finally, the popularity of gothic literature can also be attributed to its timelessness. The genre's focus on universal human emotions and desires, as well as its ability to adapt to new cultural and historical contexts, ensures that it remains relevant to audiences today. Gothic literature continues to evolve and remain popular because it appeals to our deepest fears, desires, and curiosities, and it provides a space for readers to explore these emotions in a safe and imaginative manner.

In conclusion, despite progress in science, gothic literature remains popular because it appeals to our fundamental human emotions and desires, deals with relevant themes, and has a timeless quality that ensures its continued relevance.
