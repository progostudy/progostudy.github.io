21. if no _____ has beeb obtained or covered, no work is being done.​


WORK

Answer:

If no displacement has been obtained or covered, no work is being done.

Explanation:

Energy is the capacity to do work and work is a concept in physics that tells us the transfer of energy as the object moves at a specific displacement when force is applied. Force and displacement are important factors affecting work.

A very simple example is when you push a box and it moved at a certain distance. As you push the box, you apply a certain amount of force that moved it away from its original position. However, if you push a wall with a certain amount of force and the wall did not move, you have not done any work. Your hands may hurt as you push the wall, but the energy you exerted was not transferred to the wall and did not cause it to move. This has been a common misconception since one assumes that if one exerts force, work is being done already on the object.

Also, work is a vector quantity, thus, the quantity involves magnitude and direction. It is important to note that the direction of the movement of the object should be the same as the direction of the force. In the example given earlier, as you push the box forward, for it to be considered that work is being done, the box should move forward also. However, if you are carrying a bag as you walk at a certain distance, we can say that no work is being done. The explanation for this is that the direction of the displacement of the bag is not the same as the direction of the force you are applying to the bag.

Mathematically, work can be described as W= Fd, where W is the work being done with the unit J or Nm; F is the force with the unit N, and d is the displacement with the unit m. For example, if the 10-kg box experiences a horizontal force of 50N and moving it 20m horizontally, how much work is being done? Using the formula given (W=Fd), you can solve for work. Substituting the values,

    W = Fd

        = (50N) (20m)

        = 100 Nm or 100J

There are many examples around us where we can apply the concept of work. Some examples of situations that work are evident: mom pushing a grocery cart, daddy lifting the baby, the ball falling from a table, or rocket accelerating in the sky. In all these examples, there is force exerted and there is the displacement of the object which is the same as the direction of the force.
