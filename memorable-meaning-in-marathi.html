Ductility is a mechanical property of materials that refers to their ability to deform under tensile stress without breaking. A ductile material can be drawn into thin wires or stretched into thin sheets without breaking. Metals like copper, silver, and gold are examples of highly ductile materials.

Heat, on the other hand, is a form of energy that can be transferred from one body to another due to a temperature difference. The transfer of heat can occur through conduction, convection, or radiation. In materials science and engineering, heat is often used to modify the properties of materials. For example: heat treatment can be used to increase the strength or ductility of certain metals.
