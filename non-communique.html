6. can metallic ions be replaced by non metallic ions inside batteries?​


Answer:

Abstract

The co-insertion of dual ions can often offer enhanced electrochemical performance for the aqueous zinc batteries. Although the insertion of non-metallic ions has been achieved in aqueous zinc batteries, the co-insertion chemistry of non-metallic cations is still a challenge. Here, a reversible H+/NH4+ co-insertion/extraction mechanism was developed in an aqueous Zn/MnO2 battery system. The synergistic effect between the dual cations endows the aqueous batteries with the fast kinetics of ion diffusion and the reversible structure evolution of MnO2. As a result, the Zn/MnO2 battery displays excellent rate capability and cycling performance. This work will pave the way toward the design of aqueous rechargeable batteries with non-metallic ions.

Non-porous Surfaces:

They are mainly smooth surfaces of which the latent print resides on the surface. Examples of non-porous surfaces include glass, plastics, metals, and varnished wood. Latent prints, (IE: Fingerprints),on non-porous surfaces tend to be fragile, so they must be preserved as soon as possible. The primary technique is to expose the non-porous surface to superglue fumes. Superglue reacts with moisture present in the latent print, converting the print into a hard plastic, and bonds it to the surface. Then it is treated with a fluorescent dye stain and studied under a laser.

Porous Surfaces

These are surfaces of which the latent print is absorbed into the material. Examples include paper, cardboard, and untreated woods. Several different chemical treatments are used to find prints in porous materials. The chemicals target certain amino acids that are in the print residue, reacting as a color change, making the print visible. The surface is treated with these chemicals and studied under a laser. If prints are there, they will be visible under the laser.
