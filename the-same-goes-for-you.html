18. You are on an island and you have two coconuts. The coconuts are launched with the same initial speed. The trajectory of coconut A is such that the coconut goes very high, but falls not much farther from the spot it was launched. The trajectory of coconut B is such that coconut B goes not as high as coconut A, but falls much farther from the spot it was launched. Explain with the idea of projectile motion which coconut hits the ground first.


Answer:

Coconut B hits the ground first

Explanation:

This question requires that we determine which projectile hits the ground first.

We can therefore use an equation for projectile motion that gives us the time of flight.

[tex]T = \frac{2V_{0}sin(\theta) }{g}[/tex]

In the above equation [tex]V_{0}[/tex] is the initial velocity of the projectile, g is the acceleration due to gravity and [tex]\theta[/tex] is the angle of launch for the projectile.

Since g and [tex]V_{0}[/tex] is the same for both projectiles then [tex]\theta[/tex] is the variable that makes the difference in this case. A higher value of [tex]sin(\theta)[/tex]  increases our time of flight T.

The values of [tex]sin(\theta)[/tex] are such that [tex]sin(\theta)[/tex] is near to 1 as [tex]\theta[/tex] approaches 90°, which implies that the closer the angle of launch is to 90, the longer the time of flight.

The second part is to determine which projectile has the lowest angle of projection.

We can take a look at an equation for determining the maximum height of a projectile since that is also given as part of the information.

[tex]H = \frac{V_{0} sin(\theta)}{2g}[/tex]

In the above equation we note that H is higher for values of [tex]sin\theta[/tex] closer to 1, this also implies that a projectile with an angle of project closer to 90° will go higher.

Therefore coconut B, with a lower angle of projection and longer range hits the ground before coconut A, from the above inferences
