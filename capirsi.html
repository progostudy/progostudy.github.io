12. What are the challenge in the preparation of CAPRIS and CAFSIS?.


CAPRIS and CAFSIS are systems that are used to collect, analyze, and disseminate data on agricultural production, trade, and food security. There are several challenges that may be encountered in the preparation of these systems:

1. Data collection: One of the main challenges in preparing CAPRIS and CAFSIS is collecting accurate and reliable data. This can be difficult due to issues such as a lack of reliable data sources, incomplete data, or inconsistencies in data reporting.

2. Data analysis: Another challenge is analyzing the data in a way that is useful for decision-making. This may involve dealing with large amounts of data and finding ways to effectively summarize and present the information.

3. Data dissemination: Disseminating the data to stakeholders in a timely and effective manner can also be a challenge. This may involve developing appropriate reporting tools and ensuring that the data is easily accessible to those who need it.

4. Data security: Ensuring the security and confidentiality of the data is also important. This may involve implementing appropriate measures to protect against unauthorized access or breaches of data security.

5. Funding: Sustaining and improving CAPRIS and CAFSIS can also be challenging, as it may require ongoing funding to support data collection, analysis, and dissemination efforts.

16. which of the following instrument is an example of chordophonesA.HaegumB.NokanC.PiriD.Sho​


Answer:

A

Explanation:

The haegeum (Korean: 해금) is a traditional Korean string instrument, resembling a vertical fiddle with two strings; derived from the ancient Chinese xiqin. It has a rodlike neck, a hollow wooden soundbox, and two silk strings, and is held vertically on the knee of the performer and played with a bow. It is also popularly known as kkangkkang-i (깡깡이),[1] kkaengkkaeng-i (깽깽이), or aeng-geum (앵금).[2]
