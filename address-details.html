13. What are the top 3 contagious diseases in ASIA?Give the Details in the following:DefinitionFactors on how to communicate the diseaseSteps on how to address and control itAnd 3 trivia's on each selected contagious diseases​


Answer:

The top 3 contagious diseases in Asia are:

Tuberculosis (TB)

Influenza (flu)

Measles

Here are the details for each disease:

Tuberculosis (TB):

Definition: Tuberculosis (TB) is a bacterial infection that mainly affects the lungs but can also affect other parts of the body. TB is spread through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes.

Factors on how to communicate the disease: TB is spread through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes. It can also be spread by sharing contaminated needles or through close contact with an infected person.

Steps on how to address and control it: TB can be addressed and controlled through early detection, prompt treatment, and effective public health measures such as contact tracing, isolation of infectious patients, and improved living conditions.

Trivia:

TB is one of the top 10 causes of death worldwide.

TB is more common in developing countries, including many countries in Asia.

TB can be treated with a combination of antibiotics over a period of several months.

Influenza (flu):

Definition: Influenza, commonly known as the flu, is a viral infection that affects the respiratory system. It is highly contagious and spreads easily from person to person.

Factors on how to communicate the disease: The flu is spread through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes. It can also be spread by touching a surface contaminated with the virus and then touching one's mouth or nose.

Steps on how to address and control it: The flu can be addressed and controlled through annual vaccination, good hygiene practices such as frequent handwashing, and staying home when sick.

Trivia:

Influenza viruses can mutate rapidly, which is why flu vaccines need to be updated every year.

The flu can cause serious complications in certain populations, including young children, older adults, and people with underlying health conditions.

The flu can be treated with antiviral medications if taken early in the course of the illness.

Measles:

Definition: Measles is a highly contagious viral infection that can be serious or even fatal. It is spread through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes.

Factors on how to communicate the disease: Measles is highly contagious and can be spread through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes. It can also be spread by touching a surface contaminated with the virus and then touching one's mouth or nose.

Steps on how to address and control it: Measles can be addressed and controlled through vaccination, good hygiene practices such as frequent handwashing, and staying home when sick.

Trivia:

Measles can cause serious complications, including pneumonia and encephalitis.

Measles was declared eliminated in the United States in 2000, but outbreaks still occur due to imported cases.

The MMR vaccine, which protects against measles, mumps, and rubella, is highly effective and safe

1. Influenza

Definition: Influenza, commonly known as the flu, is a contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses.

Factors on how to communicate the disease: The flu is spread from person to person through respiratory droplets that are released when an infected person talks, coughs, or sneezes. It can also be spread by touching a surface contaminated with the virus and then touching one's mouth, nose, or eyes.

Steps on how to address and control it: Vaccination is the best way to prevent the flu. Other steps include frequent hand washing, covering one's mouth and nose when coughing or sneezing, staying home when sick, and avoiding close contact with sick people.

Trivia:

Influenza viruses can mutate rapidly, making it difficult to develop effective vaccines.

The Spanish flu pandemic of 1918, which originated in Asia, is estimated to have killed between 50 and 100 million people worldwide.

The flu season in Asia typically runs from October to March.

2. Tuberculosis

Definition: Tuberculosis (TB) is a bacterial infection that primarily affects the lungs but can also affect other parts of the body.

Factors on how to communicate the disease: TB is spread from person to person through the air when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks.

Steps on how to address and control it: TB is treatable with antibiotics, but treatment can take several months to complete. Other steps to control TB include identifying and treating infected individuals, contact tracing, and improving living conditions to reduce transmission.

Trivia:

TB is one of the top 10 causes of death worldwide, and Asia has the highest burden of TB cases.

A vaccine for TB, called the Bacille Calmette-Guérin (BCG) vaccine, is available and is often given to infants in high TB burden countries.

TB can also be spread by consuming unpasteurized milk from infected animals.

3. Hepatitis A:

Definition: Hepatitis A is a contagious liver disease caused by the hepatitis A virus (HAV). It can range from mild illness to a severe, sometimes fatal, disease. Symptoms can include fever, fatigue, loss of appetite, stomach pain, nausea, dark urine, clay-colored stool, joint pain, and jaundice.

Factors on how to communicate the disease: HAV is spread by consuming contaminated food or water or having close contact with someone who is infected.

Steps on how to address and control it: Vaccinations are the most effective way to prevent the spread of HAV. It is also important to practice good hygiene, such as thoroughly washing your hands after using the bathroom, before and after handling food, and after contact with someone who is infected. Practice safe food and water preparation techniques, such as boiling and filtering.

Trivia:

- Hepatitis A is one of the most common forms of viral hepatitis

- People who travel to areas with poor sanitation are at an increased risk for hepatitis A infection

- Vaccines for hepatitis A are available for those at higher risk of infection or those traveling to areas with a high prevalence.

Suggestions to solve and address unemployment problem:

(i) Change in industrial technique:

Production technique should suit the needs and means of the country. It is essential that labour intensive technology should be encouraged in place of capital intensive technology.
