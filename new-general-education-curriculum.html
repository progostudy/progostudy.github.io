27. Research on the legality of physical education in the curriculum...​


Physical education (PE) is generally considered to be a mandatory part of the curriculum in most public schools in the United States. The Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) require that students with disabilities be provided with appropriate physical education. However, some states have different requirements for PE. In some states, homeschooled students are not required to participate in physical education. Additionally, there are some private schools that do not include PE in their curriculum. In general, physical education is considered an important part of a well-rounded education, and its inclusion in the curriculum is supported by a wide range of organizations, including the National Association for Sport and Physical Education (NASPE) and the American Heart Association (AHA).
