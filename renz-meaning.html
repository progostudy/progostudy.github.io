7. Renz wrote a poem for me.5. Present tense:6. Future tense:​


Definition of Future Perfect Tense

Future Perfect Tense represents an action which will have been occurred at some time in the future. If two actions take place in the future, the first one is Future Perfect Tense and the second one is Simple Present Tense.

Future Perfect Tense Examples

I will have written articles on different topics before you come.

Robert will have read various kinds of books.

They will have played football in that field before you reach.

April will have gone to the coffee shop before she comes here.

Bob will have gone to the library before he comes to the class.

I will have helped him to do the task before the class starts.

We will have enjoyed the musical drama before the game show starts.

I will have arranged all the nece

Present tense:
RENZ IS WRITING A POEM FOR ME

Future tense:
RENZ WILL WRITE A POEM FOR ME

8. Ms. Santos asked renz to draw all the diagonals of a certian polygon on the blackboard. Renz was able to 27 diagonal which His teacher declared correct. What was the given polygon?


Number of diagonals = [n(n-3)]/2     where n = number of sides of the polygon

Find the number of sides, n:

27 = [(n (n-3)]/2

27 = (n² - 3n) / 2

n² - 3n = 27 (2)

n² - 3n - 54 = 0

Factor the quadratic equation:

(n - 9) (n + 6) = 0

n - 9 = 0            n + 6 = 0
n = 9                 n = - 6

Choose the positive n:

n = 9
Number of sides = 9
Polygon with 9 sides = Nonagon

ANSWER:  The polygon was nonagon.

29. If Renz drives 80 kilometers in x + 5hours, what is his average speed? ​


Renz's average speed is 80/(x + 5) km/h

The average speed formula is expressed below:

[tex]\boxed{\displaystyle speed=\frac{distance}{time}}[/tex]

Given that [tex]distance=80 \ km[/tex] and [tex]time=x+5 \ hr.[/tex] Substitute them to the formula

[tex]\boxed{\displaystyle speed=\frac{80}{x+5} \ km/hr}[/tex]

The fraction cannot be further simplified, so the answer is 80/(x + 5) km/h.
