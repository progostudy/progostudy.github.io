10. Definition, strategies, and importance of the following:• Viewing• Pre-viewing• During viewing• After viewing​


Answer:

Viewing, pre-viewing, during viewing, and after viewing are terms used in the context of media consumption, particularly watching videos or films.

Viewing: This term simply refers to the act of watching a video or film.

Pre-viewing: Pre-viewing refers to the activities that happen before actually watching the video or film. It includes tasks such as reading the synopsis or summary, researching the cast and crew, or watching trailers to get an idea of what the video or film is about. Pre-viewing helps the viewer to better understand the context of the video or film and can also help them make an informed decision about whether to watch it or not.

During viewing: During viewing refers to the activities that happen while watching the video or film. This can include things like taking notes, discussing the content with others, or analyzing the cinematography, acting, or plot. During viewing helps the viewer to engage more deeply with the video or film and can also help them to better understand and appreciate it.

After viewing: After viewing refers to the activities that happen after the video or film has ended. This can include things like reflecting on what was seen, discussing the content with others, or writing a review. After viewing allows the viewer to process what they have seen and can also help them to form opinions and make decisions about the video or film.

Explanation:

Overall, these four stages of media consumption are important because they help the viewer to engage more deeply with the content and get more out of the experience. By taking the time to preview, engage during the viewing, and reflect after the fact, viewers can gain a better understanding and appreciation of the video or film, and make more informed decisions about what to watch in the future.
