1. definition of pulmonary tuberculosis​


Answer:

Pulmonary TB is a bacterial infection of the lungs that can cause a range of symptoms, including chest pain, breathlessness, and severe coughing. Pulmonary TB can be life-threatening if a person does not receive treatment. People with active TB can spread the bacteria through the air.

Answer:

Pulmonary tuberculosis (TB) is a contagious bacterial infection that involves the lungs. It may spread to other organs.

If you have pulmonary TB disease, your doctor may prescribe several medicines. You’ll need to take these drugs for six months or longer for the best results.

9. Activity 4: Group Us UpFill up the table, comparing systemic circulation from pulmonary circulation.Pulmonary CirculationSystemic Circulation pasagot naman po plsssssss. ​


Answer:

Pulmonary circulation moves blood between the heart and the lungs. It transports deoxygenated blood to the lungs to absorb oxygen and release carbon dioxide. The oxygenated blood then flows back to the heart. Systemic circulation moves blood between the heart and the rest of the body

Systemic carries oxygenated blood from aorta to tissues. Pulmonary carries deoxygenated blood/venous blood to lungs then back to the heart. In systemic, the L ventricle ejects blood into aorta. In pulmonary, the R ventricle ejects blood into pulmonary circulation.

Here are the signs and symptoms and effects of the common communicable diseases you listed:

Acute Respiratory infections

Signs and symptoms: cough, sore throat, fever, runny or stuffy nose, headache, body aches

Effects: fatigue, difficulty breathing, complications like pneumonia or bronchitis

Pneumonia

Signs and symptoms: high fever, cough, shortness of breath, chest pain, chills, fatigue, sweating

Effects: difficulty breathing, organ failure, sepsis, death

Bronchitis

Signs and symptoms: cough, chest discomfort, shortness of breath, fatigue, fever, chills

Effects: difficulty breathing, pneumonia, worsening of asthma, death (in severe cases)

Influenza

Signs and symptoms: fever, cough, sore throat, runny or stuffy nose, body aches, headache, chills, fatigue

Effects: complications like pneumonia or bronchitis, hospitalization, death (in severe cases)

Pulmonary Tuberculosis (PTB)

Signs and symptoms: persistent cough, fatigue, weight loss, fever, night sweats, chest pain, coughing up blood

Effects: weakened immune system, spread of infection to other organs, death (in severe cases)

Dengue

Signs and symptoms: high fever, severe headache, joint and muscle pain, fatigue, nausea, vomiting, skin rash

Effects: severe bleeding, shock, organ failure, death (in severe cases)

Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIS)

Signs and symptoms: varies depending on the type of infection but may include pain or discomfort during sex, discharge from genitals, itching or burning around genitals, rash or sores on genitals or mouth, flu-like symptoms

Effects: infertility, increased risk of certain cancers, complications during pregnancy, transmission to partners

HIV and AIDS

Signs and symptoms: flu-like symptoms (during early stages), weight loss, fever, night sweats, fatigue, swollen lymph nodes, persistent cough

Effects: weakened immune system, increased risk of infections and certain cancers, death (in severe cases)

24. Which is NOT true about communicable diseases? A. Antibiotics always cure pneumonia. B. Pulmonary tuberculosis can be treated. C. People do not need to get vaccinated if they are healthy. D. Children are more vulnerable to bronchitis as compared to adults.​


Answer:

Option C is not true about communicable diseases. People need to get vaccinated even if they are healthy because vaccines help in building immunity against infectious diseases. Vaccines contain weakened or dead microorganisms that stimulate the immune system to produce antibodies, which provide protection against the disease.

Antibiotics do not always cure pneumonia as the effectiveness of antibiotics depends on the type of bacteria causing the infection. Some bacteria may be resistant to certain antibiotics, making them ineffective in treating the infection.

Pulmonary tuberculosis can be treated with a combination of antibiotics for a period of six months or more. However, treatment may be complicated by drug-resistant strains of tuberculosis, which require longer treatment periods and more potent medications.

Children are more vulnerable to bronchitis as their immune systems are still developing, and they have smaller airways that can easily become blocked by mucus. Adults are less susceptible to bronchitis as their immune systems are stronger and they have larger airways.
