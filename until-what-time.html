2. What is Prepositions of time on for, since, during and until


Prepositions of Time

SINCE : + time / point in the past (yesterday, last year)

FOR : + period of time (2 hours/3 days/1 year)

UNTIL : up to a point in time

TILL : Less formal way of saying until

FROM….TO : Use from for the start time and to for the finish time

4. what major political thoughts have emerged since the ancient times until the present? How are these relevant to our study of politics and governance? ​


Answer:

Mankind has always tried to identify the best method of organizing themselves into productive, cohesive societies. The difficulty has been, and remains so, accounting for the disparity in talents and circumstances of each individual without resorting to violence.

In other words, we employ “politics” as an alternative to killing each other.

Countless ideologies have informed the establishment and operation of governments and societies throughout human history, but each of them can be appraised reliably using one simple filter - how does it account for the darker elements of human nature?

There are only two sorts of people in this world: those who want to be let alone, to live as they choose, and those who simply cannot keep their hands to themselves.

This distinction lies behind every ideological conflict in history.

Those of the former sort believe in individual worth and sovereignty as preeminent values, subsequently choosing government structures that restrain the power of the state, while the latter variety try to shortcut human behavioral management by empowering the state (as representative of the collective) to order society in a “fair” and “just” fashion.

Those who prefer to restrain government recognize that mankind is not “perfectible,” and becomes more (not less) dangerous in groups while the more activist minded believe behavior to be a mere construct, subject to their “perfecting” influence.

Who’s definition of “fair” and “just” shall be used?

It is in dealing with those who believe differently from the state-approved notions that has resulted in collectivist ideologies killing more of their own people than even the most brutal dictatorships in the aggregate.

There is no substitute for the concept of enlightened self-interest as a means of ordering society to its greatest potential. Only by hundreds of millions of individual choices being made daily can we offset the far fewer choices of those who are led by their darker impulses.

An achievement no top-down centrally-planned government could possibly match.
