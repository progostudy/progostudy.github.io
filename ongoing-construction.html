 Goals

The goal to be achieved is to provide a more complete explanation of the Psychodynamic Nursing theory developed by Hildegard E. Peplau.

 

Understanding Psychodynamic Nursing Theory

The theory developed by Hildegard E. Peplau is called psychodynamic nursing. This theory is influenced by the model of interpersonal relationships that are therapeutic (significant therapeutic interpersonal process).

Hildegard E. Peplau defines psychodynamic nursing theory as follows: “Psychodynamic nursing is being able to understand one’s own behavior to help others identify felt difaculties and to apply principles of human relations to the problems that arise at all levels of experience.”

According to Peplau, psychodynamic treatment is the ability to understand a person's behavior to help identify perceived difficulties and apply human principles related to problems that arise from all things or events that have been experienced.
