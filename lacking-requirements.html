2. A lack of essential in vitamins and minerals, that required to sustain the needed food in a daily diet.​


STATE OF LACK OF ESSENTIAL IN VITAMINS AND MINERALS

Micronutrient deficiencies are described as a lack of essential vitamins and minerals required to sustain the needed food in a daily diet. Micronutrient deficiencies are also a term for having not enough nutrient amounts by the body for proper growth and development.

What causes and effects of micronutrient deficiency?

A lack of one or more of the micronutrient essential for good plant or animal health is known as micronutrient deficiency or dietary deficiency. Deficiencies of critical trace minerals are referred to as vitamin shortages in humans and other animals, whereas vitamin deficiencies are referred to as mineral deficiencies in plants.

The building blocks of optimal health are vitamins and minerals, often known as micronutrients. Micronutrient malnutrition, which can be fatal, occurs when people do not get enough of these critical nutrients. Serious birth deformities, a lack of cognitive development, and a decrease in productivity are some of the consequences. Maternal and newborn fatalities, as well as childhood blindness, are linked to severe micronutrient deficiency.

It has a significant impact on a person's health, and a lack of any of them can result in serious and even life-threatening illnesses. They help the body manufacture enzymes, hormones, and other compounds that are necessary for appropriate growth and development, among other things.

Deficits in micronutrients can lead to evident and hazardous health problems, but they can also contribute to less clinically noticeable decreases in energy, mental clarity, and general capacity, among other things.

As a result, educational performance, work productivity, and the risk of various diseases and health problems can all be harmed as a result of this.

Nutrition education and the consumption of a varied diet, as well as food fortification and supplementation where necessary, can help to prevent many of these deficiencies. In recent decades, these programs have made significant progress in lowering micronutrient deficiencies, but more work is needed.

7. "BARRIERS"Lack of TimeSocial InfluenceLack of EnergyLack of MotivationFear of InjuryLack of SkillLack of ResourcesWeather Conditions, Travel or Vacation."Ways to Overcome Barriers to Sports Participation"​


Answer:

Lack of time

Add physical activity to your daily routine. For example, walk or ride your bike to work or shopping, organize school activities around physical activity, walk the dog, take the stairs, exercise while you watch TV, park farther away from your destination, etc.

Social Influence

Invite friends and family members to exercise with you. Plan social activities involving exercise. Develop new friendships with physically active people. Join a group, such as the YMCA or a hiking club.

Lack of energy

1.Schedule physical activity for times in the day or week when you feel energetic.

2.Convince yourself that if you give it a chance, physical activity will increase your energy level; then, try it.

Lack of motivation

1. Plan ahead. Make physical activity a regular part of your daily or weekly schedule and write it on your calendar.

2. Invite a friend to exercise with you on a regular basis and write it on both your calendars.

3. Join an exercise group or class.

Fear of injury

1. Learn how to warm up and cool down to prevent injury.

2. Learn how to exercise appropriately considering your age, fitness level, skill level, and health status.

3. Choose activities you feel you can do safely, and increase the amount you do gradually as your confidence and abilities grow.

Lack of clarity:

Lack of creativity:
