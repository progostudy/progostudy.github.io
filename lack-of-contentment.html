3. What is the meaning of lack of contentment


What is Lack of Contentment?

Lack of Contentment is being not fulfilled or unsatisfied with the things that you have possessed. Being not contented is being jealous of what others have owned. Instead of admiring others, you become competitive with them. Soon, you start to hate yourself and strive to possess more than you currently have.

Negative Effects of Lack of Contentment:

Humiliation Permanent anxiety Miserable life Envy Unsatisfaction Hatred Greediness Corruption Theft Discouragement Lack of trust Despair Manifest Pride Rebellion Low self-esteem

Reasons why you are Lacking Contentment :

You have hate and resentment in your heart. You do not value your relationship with God. You don't have enough inspiration. You don't know your priorities in life. You are looking for happiness in the wrong places.  

Ways to Overcome Lack of Contentment :

Change your mindset and attitude. Get some manner of positive behavior. Begin with something that provides you with purpose. Enjoy the things you only have. Choosing to get control is a determination to value ourselves. Spend time with people you love. Start thinking that you can make things better. Work harder to attain your goals. Find good in everything.  Enjoy every moment of your life. Help the lives of others to become better.

24. Write 10 effect of lack of contentment


Answer:

10 Effects of Lack of Contentment:Greed - when a person thinks that he/she does not have enough, they tend to desire for more. Most of the time, the desire would make you go blind and greed will take place. People cares less about anything just to satisfy their greed.Envy and Jealousy - a person who is not contented will look at other people and see what they have that they don't. Insecurities surround a person who is not contented.Cheating is encouraged - since a person has limits, we really can't have it all. But some people does not want to be stopped by these limits and resort to cheating to cover up imperfections.Theft - this is also an effect of envy. When you aren't contented with what you have, and you see something that you desire but can't do anything to have it, your desire will push you to steal it. Theft is not only with material things, but also exists with roles, achievements, and others.Corruption - this could be quite similar to theft. But this effect could be described or defined in the higher degree that results to more damages. Corruption may include fooling other people, aside from the theft itself.No peace of mind - being a person who isn't satisfied with what they have, you always think of a way to have what you want. You become restless and wouldn't have a peace of mind.Illegal business/activities - when a person wanted to fulfill something out of their dissatisfaction, they will do all means of acquiring it even if it's through illegal acts.Losing important people - being focused too much in what you don't have will eventually make you lose the people that really matter. You always seek for ways to have what you want while you fail to keep what you already have.Greater risks - strong desire to acquire something is what follows a lack of contentment. Through this, decision-making will be affected by the strong emotion and makes someone more irrational and would increase risks on health, relationships, fortune and other aspects.Depression - when people tried everything just to satisfy themselves but still didn't feel satisfaction, they will be depressed. Lack of contentment is endless, and it does not stop by fulfiiling just one desire. It keeps coming and when time comes that there is no way to fulfill that desire, you will never be contented.

26. example of essay about lack of contentment


Contentment is the state of being satisfied with one's lot. The contented man is pleased with what he has. He does not make himself miserable by envying those who have what he has not. Such contentment breeds happiness. It is, therefore, worth cultivating; and this can be done.Discontent is an ugly thing. It brings nothing but unhappiness. A discontented man is unhappy himself, and his constant grumbling and bad temper make all round him unhappy too. He is always comparing his lot with the lot of those better off than himself. His envy of them makes him bitter. He hates others for their greater success and prosper­ity. He is a miserable wretch. So we must value every thing we have and be grateful because compare to the other people, we are very lucky
