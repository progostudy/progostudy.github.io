9. Japanese values/traditions in the story of the aged mother


The despotic leader made an order to kill the old people. There was a poor farmer and his old mother living at the foot of the mountain. The father of the poor farmer is dead so it is just the two of them.      He carried his mother to the top of the mountain to be left dying. While climbing the mountain, he left twigs to mark the path for his way back. The poor farmer loves his mother so much and he can not leave her.      That is why he kept and hid her beneath their kitchen cabinet. The despotic leader ordered the people to make a rope of ashes. His mother advised him to lay the a twisted rope on the flat rock and burned it on the windless night.      He was successful in making a rope of ashes. The leader got to know what the old lady taught him. Ultimately, the leader told his nation that he won’t order to kill the elderly anymore.     The leader realized that the elderly has the wisdom so they are indispensible.        The Japanese upholds the values and traditions of following and respecting the elderly. The story shows that Japanese are obedient, as the poor man listened to what his mother said. They also depicted the values of love and reverence.
