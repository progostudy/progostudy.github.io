9. cow color can be white cw or white cb. mixing white and black makes spotted cows CWCB. A black cow and a spotted cow mate. What offspring could they have at what possibilities


Answer:

50% spotted

50% black

Explanation:

In order to determine the offspring, we do what is call the Punnett Square which is a square diagram which allows us to organize the possible different genotypes.

A black cow's genotype is [tex]C^BC^B[/tex] while the spotted cow's is [tex]C^WC^B[/tex].

           [tex]C^W[/tex]            [tex]C^B[/tex]

[tex]C^B[/tex]      [tex]C^WC^B[/tex]       [tex]C^BC^B[/tex]

[tex]C^B[/tex]      [tex]C^WC^B[/tex]       [tex]C^BC^B[/tex]

The offspring have the ratio [tex]2C^WC^B : 2C^BC^B[/tex], this would correspond to the phenotypes 2 spotted : 2 black cows. Each have an equal chance so 50% spotted and 50% black.
