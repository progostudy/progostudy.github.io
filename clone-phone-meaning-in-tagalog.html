Answer:

Cloning is a technique scientists use to make exact genetic copies of living things. Genes, cells, tissues, and even whole animals can all be cloned.

Some clones already exist in nature. Single-celled organisms like bacteria make exact copies of themselves each time they reproduce. In humans, identical twins are similar to clones. They share almost the exact same genes. Identical twins are created when a fertilized egg splits in two.

Scientists also make clones in the lab. They often clone genes in order to study and better understand them. To clone a gene, researchers take DNA from a living creature and insert it into a carrier like bacteria or yeast. Every time that carrier reproduces, a new copy of the gene is made.

Animals are cloned in one of two ways. The first is called embryo twinning. Scientists first split an embryo in half. Those two halves are then placed in a mother’s uterus. Each part of the embryo develops into a unique animal, and the two animals share the same genes. The second method is called somatic cell nuclear transfer. Somatic cells are all the cells that make up an organism, but that are not sperm or egg cells. Sperm and egg cells contain only one set of chromosomes, and when they join during fertilization, the mother’s chromosomes merge with the father’s. Somatic cells, on the other hand, already contain two full sets of chromosomes. To make a clone, scientists transfer the DNA from an animal’s somatic cell into an egg cell that has had its nucleus and DNA removed. The egg develops into an embryo that contains the same genes as the cell donor. Then the embryo is implanted into an adult female’s uterus to grow

GENE CLONING vs NUCLEAR CLONING

Differences:

Definition: Gene cloning involves the isolation and replication of specific DNA sequences, while nuclear cloning involves the transfer of a nucleus from a donor cell into an enucleated egg cell to create a genetically identical organism.

Type of cells used: Gene cloning is typically performed using somatic cells, while nuclear cloning is performed using differentiated cells.

Process: Gene cloning involves the use of restriction enzymes and ligases to create recombinant DNA molecules, while nuclear cloning involves the removal of the nucleus from an egg cell and the introduction of a new nucleus.

Resulting organisms: Gene cloning results in genetically identical copies of the specific DNA sequence that was cloned, while nuclear cloning results in genetically identical organisms.

Applications: Gene cloning is commonly used in research to study the function of specific genes or to produce large quantities of recombinant proteins, while nuclear cloning is used to create genetically identical animals for research purposes or for agricultural purposes.

Similarities:

Both techniques involve the replication of genetic material.

Both techniques can be used to create genetically identical organisms.

Both techniques have applications in research and agriculture.

Both techniques have ethical concerns surrounding their use.

Both techniques have potential applications in human medicine.

In summary, gene cloning and nuclear cloning are two different techniques that involve the replication of genetic material, but differ in their definition, type of cells used, process, resulting organisms, and applications. However, both techniques have some similarities, such as their ability to create genetically identical organisms and their potential applications in various fields.

30. Explain the sentence:"Not all pure cultures are clone but a clone is a pure culture." ​


Answer:

The sentence "Not all pure cultures are clone but a clone is a pure culture" is referring to the concepts of pure cultures and clones in microbiology.

A pure culture is a population of identical microorganisms that are all descendants of a single cell or colony. This means that all the microorganisms in the culture have the same genetic makeup and characteristics. Pure cultures are important in microbiology because they allow researchers to study the properties and behavior of a single type of microorganism without interference from other types.

A clone, on the other hand, is a group of genetically identical cells that are derived from a single parent cell through a process called cloning. Clones can be created artificially in the laboratory or can occur naturally through processes such as asexual reproduction.

The sentence is saying that while all clones are pure cultures (because they are genetically identical), not all pure cultures are clones. This is because pure cultures can also be composed of genetically diverse microorganisms that have been separated from other types through various methods such as isolation or selection.

Answer:

A pure culture refers to a population of microorganisms, such as bacteria or fungi, that contains only a single type of organism. This means that all the cells in the culture are genetically identical and have the same characteristics, including their physical appearance and behavior.

On the other hand, a clone refers to a group of cells or organisms that are genetically identical and have been derived from a single parent cell or organism. In other words, a clone is a pure culture that has been produced by a specific method of cell division, such as mitosis or cloning.

Therefore, not all pure cultures are clones because they may have been obtained by other methods, such as isolation from a mixed culture or purification from a contaminated sample. However, a clone is always a pure culture because it is produced by a specific method that ensures the genetic identity of all the cells in the culture.
