1) An object moves as a result of force.

Unless altered by another force, a moving object maintains its constant speed or velocity.

Isaac Newton discovered the principles of motion in 1687. He realized that moving an item requires force and that once that object is going, nothing can stop it or make it travel faster or slower than another external force. An object that is not moving will not begin to move until some force is applied to it. This is known as the law of inertia. On Earth, where gravity and friction play a role in moving objects, this and Newton's other laws are difficult to see.

2) Newton’s Three Laws of MotionLAW 1 - Nothing occurs until something occurs to it. An item that is neither pushed or pulled either remains stationary or moves in a straight line at a constant pace. Nothing moves unless acted upon by a force. If something is moving, it will continue to move at the same pace and in the same direction unless it is changed by a force.LAW 2 - Forces accelerate things (or accelerate). When a force pushes or pulls on an object, it moves in the same direction as the force. The greater the acceleration, the larger the force and the lighter the object. It can also cause anything to slow down, accelerate, or shift direction.LAW 3 - Every action has a corresponding and opposing reaction. When a force acts in one direction, it generates an equal and opposing force.

3) Heavier (more mass) items are more difficult to move and stop.

Heavier items (those with a higher mass) are more resistant to change than lighter objects. Pushing a bicycle or a Cadillac, for example, or stopping them once they are moving. The more heavier the thing (the greater its inertia), the more difficult it is to start or stop.
