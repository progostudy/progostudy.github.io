It examines the ways in which women are portrayed, and how their experiences and perspectives are either marginalized or privileged in the literary selection.Philosophical Criticism is a literary approach that examines the underlying philosophical ideas and concepts presented in literature, and how they relate to broader intellectual and cultural debates. It explores the meanings and implications of the text's themes, symbols, and characters, and how they reflect different philosophical perspectives.Historical Criticism is a literary approach that focuses on the historical context in which the literary selection was produced and received. It examines how the text reflects the cultural, social, and political climate of its time, and how it is influenced by historical events, movements, and ideas.Historical/Biographical Criticism is a literary approach that looks at the life and times of the author of the literary selection, and how these factors influence the text. It considers the author's experiences, beliefs, and cultural background, and how they are reflected in the literary work. It also examines the historical context in which the author lived, and how it shapes the literary selection.
