Trichanthera is a monotypic genus of flowering plants in the acanthus family containing the single species Tricanthera gigantea, which is known by many common names, including madre de agua, suiban, cenicero, tuno, naranjillo, and palo de agua. It is native to Central America and Northern Southern America. It has also been introduced to other tropical regions such as Vietnam, Cambodia, and the Philippines.

It is bell-shaped and the throat measured up to 2.5 centimeters long. The plant bloom in the afternoon and the flowers fall away during the night. The flowers are pollinated by bats. The bat Glossophaga soricina has been seen at the flowers of this species.

Given:

A solution is prepared by dissolving 90 mL of hydrogen peroxide in enough water to make 3000 mL of solution.

To find:

Identify the concentration of the hydrogen peroxide solution.

Solution:

We use the formula, Volume percent = Volume of solute / Volume of solution × 100 %

From given, we have the parameters,

The volume of solute is 90 mL

The volume of solution is 3000 mL

Substitute the values in the given formula,

Volume percent = Volume of solute / Volume of solution × 100 %

= 90 mL / 3000mL × 100 %

∴ The volume percent = 3 %
