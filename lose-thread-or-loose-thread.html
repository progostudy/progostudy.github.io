Looping stitches are caused by?

Looping Stitches: What Causes It?

Improper tension is the most common cause of looped threads. If the loop is on the upper side, releasing the top tension or tightening the lower tension may be used to rectify it. If the loop is on the underside, the best way to fix it is to adjust the upper tension. (Note: Some newer machines have factory-set bobbins that should not be changed; consult your machine's manual for further information.)

Make sure the upper and lower threading is perfect, as well as that the needle is of good quality and the right size for the thread.

The incorrect placement of the bobbin in the bobbin casing might result in stitch looping. For instructions on tension on how to insert the bobbin, consult your machine's handbook.

11. Fill in the blanks with the correct answer. Write your answer in the answer sheetprovided.1.____is the thread holder.2.____keeps the thread in position3.____releases the thread and interlocks with the bobbin thread.4.____moves the presser foot.5.____the looseness and tightness of stitches.6.___holds the needle in place.7.____holds and tightens the needle.8.____holds the fabric in place while sewing9.____is a slender tool attached in the needle clamp usedfor sewing10.____controls the bobbin while winding thread​


Answer:

1. Thread Holder

We call it a sewing machine thread stand or a sewing thread holder. A stand for one or more spools of sewing-thread, on which the spools are mounted on pins, so as to turn freely as the thread is unwound. Also spool-stand .

2. Thread Guide

Thread guides- holds the thread in place on the sewing machine.

3. Thread Take up Lever

Thread Take up Lever -releases the thread and interlocks with the bobbin thread.

4. Feed Dog

Feed dog. Teeth that move the fabric under the presser foot, advancing one stitch at a time.

5. Tension Control

The part of the sewing machine that controls the looseness and tightness stitches is the tension control.

6. Needle Clamp

Needle clamp-holds the needle in place.

7. Needle Clamp

Needle Clamp holds and tightens the needle.

8. Presser Foot

Presser foot- holds fabric in place while you sew.

9. Needle

Needle is a slender tool attached in the needle clamp used for sewing.

10. Bobbin Winder

Bobbin winder tension- provides tension on the thread when the bobbin winds.
