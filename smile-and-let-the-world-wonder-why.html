2. do you agree with the song writters what a wonderful world why or why not?​


Answer:

Well, for a start the song was written by two people, Bob Theile and George David Weiss. I don’t know for sure but it is very likely that Theile wrote all or most of the words and Weiss wrote all or most of the music. Therefore it is likely that either the words were written first and then set to music or the music came first and the words were written for it. Which one of the songwriters originally came up with the ‘concept’ of the song I don’t know, but, it would not be right to just assume it was the guy who wrote the lyrics.

In an interview Weiss said the song was written for Louis Armstrong to sing and inspired by Armstrong’s ability to bring different races together.

Written and released in 1967 the song’s optimistic, and frankly rather naive, message was very much in keeping with the mood of the times, which were witnessing the fashionable rise of the peace & love hippie movement and its influence on mainstream culture. It was the best-selling single of 1968 in the UK, which helped its eventual success in the US where initially it had not been a hit. A fine example of a couple of middle-aged jazz-era songwriters and an elderly trad jazz performer tapping into the spirit of the times and the feelings of the younger generation, without compromising their own, by then, very unfashionable musical roots.

10. why did Louis Armstrong wrote the song what a wonderful world?


The song "What a Wonderful World" is written by late Bob Thiele and George David Weiss, not by Louis Armstrong. These two writers firstly offered the song to Tony Bennett, but he turned it down. Until it is offered to Louis Armstrong.

George Weiss then wrote the song specially for Louis Armstrong. Weiss was inspired by Armstrong's ability to bring people of different races together.

The song recorded on August 16, 1967, and released on October 18, 1967 under ABC Records. It didn't made a popularity around the United States but the song made an instant hit in the United Kingdom earning #1 at the UK Singles Chart.

Last 2014, Louis Armstrong's "What a Wonderful World" 1967 record sold more than 2 Million downloads in the United States alone after it was digitally released.
