13. what is ever gone skinny dipping means?​


to swim in the nude

especially on someone else's private land or in a public setting.

This idiom's etymology

In 1947, the phrase "skinny dipping" was first observed in use. Swimming naked was considered the norm and was performed worldwide up until this point. Due to developments in materials that could dry quickly after being wet, the practice of swimming naked was no longer thought to be required in 1947. The term "skinny dipping" was created in order to distinguish between the new and traditional ways of swimming. Skinny dipping, the practice of swimming by only dipping your skin in the water, was formerly popular. Nowadays, "skinny dipping" is frequently seen as criminal in several nations unless done in specific places.

14. An argument for consequentualism which states that actions are transient things, soon gone forever.


Select one:
a. All of the answers correct
b. Love
c. Happiness
d. Only results remain

Answer: D. ONLY RESULTS REMAIN

Explanation: Actions are transient things, soon gone forever. Hence, one might think, in the long run only the results remain, so the only thing that really matters about an action is its results.


Additional info: Consequentialism or Teleologic ethics is the philosophical view that the moral value of an act is to be judged on its consequences, rather than the act itself. Consequentialism is therefore opposed to deontological ethics.

The most well known school of consequentialism is utilitarianism.

Consequentialism is a broad term, encompassing many schools of thought and one is advised to familiarise oneself with each school to fully understand what consequentialism is.
