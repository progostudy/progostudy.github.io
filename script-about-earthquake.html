3. earthquake script roleplay ​


Answer:

Earthquake Simulation Script

Imagine that you hear a low, rumbling, roaring sound. The noise builds, getting louder and

louder, for a few seconds. Then, Wham! There’s a terrific jolt. You feel like someone suddenly

slammed on the brakes in the car, or like a truck just rammed into the side of the building.

The floor seems to be moving beneath you. It’s hard to stand up, or even stay in your seat. If you do

stand up, you might feel like you’re riding a raft down a fast river. When you walk, it’s like trying to

walk on a trampoline or a waterbed. You hear someone say, “Earthquake! Drop, Cover, and Hold!”

I want all of you at your desks to take cover as quickly and quietly as you can, right now. Please

listen very carefully.

The shaking and commotion may last about 60 seconds or a little longer. We’ll have our timer count

off the seconds for as long as this earthquake lasts. [The timer may begin counting softly now.]

The building is creaking and rattling. Books are falling from the bookcase. Hanging lamps and

plants are swaying. Suddenly a pot falls to the floor and smashes, and the plant spills. A window

pane just shattered, and glass is falling to the floor. The table is sliding, too.

Be sure to stay in the drop, cover, and hold position under your desk. If your desk is moving,

grab the legs and move with it.

You hear noises outside. Dogs are barking. Cats are meowing. A baby is crying. People are

shouting and screaming. The shaking is making church bells ring. You hear crashing sounds,

from brick chimneys and other loose parts of the building falling to the ground. Trees outside

are swaying and scraping against the walls.

Inside the room, pictures are moving on their nails. Oh! That one just fell off the wall and

crashed to the floor. The desk drawers are sliding open. The lights begin to flicker on and off...

they just went out! Now the door swings back and forth on its hinges. Bang! It slams shut. There’s

silence now. Just as suddenly as the noise and shaking began, the room grows quiet. [The timer

can stop counting now.]

Please, everyone, get back in your seats. It is important to remain very quiet and wait for

instructions. When it is safe to leave the building, I am going to lead you outside to an open space.

Stay together, and be ready to take cover again at any moment, because the shaking may start again.

Sometimes other quakes, called aftershocks, occur after the damaging earthquake has stopped.

Volunteers, Inc.

In this paper, we will be primarily examining the problem of saving human life from earthquakes. “Set me free, o, stalwart king of birds!”, was the plead. “Peck harder! I can’t breathe. It’s a confinement!”

All of a sudden, the tall bamboo broke. To the bird’s surprise, a handsome man emerged from the bamboo.

“Thanks, O, great king of the birds! My name is Malakas. Please continue pecking the bamboo. Release my partner with your grace and power!”

Once again, the bird pecked the bamboo.

14. script about the pandemic​


Answer:

During the current COVID-19 situation, all of us are wondering how Hollywood is going to resume production. I have read many of the COVID white papers published by various “experts” for the Industry, but a quick glance will reveal them either to have been penned by liability fearing attorneys or out-of-work science fiction writers.

Sure, let’s test the crew on a daily basis, get instant results, and house them all in quarantine for the duration of the shoot. My wife is a nurse at one of the most prestigious and medically state-of-the-art hospitals in the country – and THEY cannot test everyone even once!

Let’s divide all the cast and crew into “zones” and enforce that no one shall cross the line. Have these “experts” ever worked on a set? What do they think that we actually DO during a production?

Ludicrousness aside, there is no doubt that we all have to be as careful and sanitary as possible.

That brings us to the issue of “student thesis or senior projects”. If Hollywood, with all of their resources and extravagant budgets (I guess that the long list of Executive Associate Name-on-Credits Producers will need to suffer a few million dollars less per person) can barely figure out COVID protocols, then where does that leave our student filmmakers?

At the university where I teach, some administrators have recommended that students may only film on campus, on the soundstage, for not more than a few days. Mind you, the department does not even have any set construction facilities, nor offer set design/set construction. Only a few crude flats and a closet worth of props for basic television or acting/directing exercises.

Our film students, in the recent past, used to produce Hollywood quality short films shot with full student crews, rented professional equipment, SAG actors, with budgets in the $25-50,000 range. Although some of these films have won awards internationally, their primary achievement was that the students got the opportunity to learn how to make movies in the real world and to interact with local “Hollywood” resources (permit offices, insurance, casting agents, locations, labs, etc.).

So reverting to shooting crude scenes, with only one or two actors at a time, using only the limited campus resources would not be educationally fulfilling. Their graduating portfolios would look like seventh grade YouTube follies.

However, there are ways to compromise.

22. script about awtoridad


Title: The Authority

FADE IN:

INT. A GOVERNMENT OFFICE - DAY

We see a group of people sitting in a conference room. A man, JASON, stands up to speak.

JASON

Good afternoon, everyone. As you all know, I'm Jason, and I'm here to talk about the authority.

A few people nod their heads in agreement, and some start taking notes.

JASON

Authority is defined as the power or right to give orders, make decisions, and enforce obedience. It is a crucial aspect of any government or organization. Without authority, chaos would reign, and nothing would get done.

One person, SARAH, raises her hand.

SARAH

But Jason, isn't there a danger in having too much authority? It could lead to abuse of power.

JASON

That's a valid concern, Sarah. That's why there needs to be checks and balances in place to prevent any misuse of authority. It's also essential to have transparency and accountability.

Another person, MIKE, speaks up.

MIKE

But how do we ensure that those in positions of authority are qualified and competent to make decisions?

JASON

That's where meritocracy comes in. By having a merit-based system, we can ensure that those with the necessary skills and expertise are in positions of authority. It also helps to have regular training and development programs to ensure that those in authority stay up-to-date with the latest developments in their field.

A third person, LISA, chimes in.

LISA

But what about people who don't agree with those in authority? What about dissent and opposition?

JASON

Dissent and opposition are a healthy part of any democratic society. People have the right to voice their opinions and hold those in authority accountable. It's important to listen to feedback and criticisms and make necessary changes where needed.

The group nods in agreement, and there is a moment of silence.

JASON

In conclusion, authority is essential for the smooth functioning of any organization or government. It's crucial to have checks and balances, transparency, and accountability to prevent any misuse of power. By having a merit-based system and regular training and development programs, we can ensure that those in positions of authority are qualified and competent to make decisions. And finally, dissent and opposition are important and should be encouraged as part of a healthy democratic society.

FADE OUT.

25. Create an roleplay before, during and after an earthquake (15 person and a narrator)​​script​


Answer:

Narrator: Ladies and gentlemen, today we will be conducting a roleplay on how to prepare and respond during an earthquake. We have 15 participants, and we will take you through what you should do before, during, and after an earthquake. Let's get started!

(Before the Earthquake)

Narrator: It is a typical day, and all our participants are going about their daily routine. Suddenly, the ground starts shaking.

Participant 1: What is happening? Is this an earthquake?

Participant 2: Yes, it is an earthquake. Quick, everyone needs to take cover under a table or desk.

Participant 3: Wait, we should also turn off any gas or electrical appliances to prevent fires.

Participant 4: And make sure you stay away from windows and glass doors.

Participant 5: Don't forget to also secure any loose items or objects that could fall and cause injuries.

(During the Earthquake)

Narrator: The earthquake is now in full swing, and our participants are in a safe location.

Participant 6: Hold on tight, everyone. Don't panic, just stay calm.

Participant 7: Keep your head down and cover your neck with your hands.

Participant 8: Don't try to run outside or use the elevators.

Participant 9: If you're in bed, stay there and cover your head with a pillow.

Participant 10: If you're outside, move away from buildings and power lines.

(After the Earthquake)

Narrator: The earthquake has finally stopped. Our participants are safe, but the danger is not over yet.

Participant 11: Is everyone okay?

Participant 12: Let's check for injuries and help those who need it.

Participant 13: We should also be prepared for aftershocks.

Participant 14: And listen to any updates or instructions from local authorities.

Participant 15: Don't forget to also inspect your home for any damages and turn off the gas and electricity if necessary.

Narrator: And there you have it, folks. That concludes our roleplay on how to prepare and respond during an earthquake. Remember, it is always better to be safe than sorry, so make sure you know what to do in case of an earthquake. Thank you for participating!


26. script about militarismo ​


Title: Militarism: Its Effects on Society

Opening Shot: A group of soldiers in full uniform marching in a parade.

Voiceover: Militarism is a societal belief that prioritizes the military and its needs. While it may seem like a necessary aspect of national defense, militarism can have negative effects on society.

Cut to: A classroom scene where a history teacher is lecturing about the rise of militarism in a particular country.

Teacher: Militarism is often accompanied by nationalism and the belief that war is necessary to protect the country's interests. This ideology can lead to an arms race, where countries compete to have the most advanced military technology.

Cut to: A news clip showing a military parade with tanks and other weapons on display.

News Anchor: The country's leaders have stated that the military is the backbone of the nation and that it must be strong to protect the country from external threats.

Cut to: A family dinner where a parent is discussing their child's future.

Parent: I think joining the military would be a good career choice for you. You'll learn discipline and valuable skills that will benefit you in the future.

Cut to: A soldier in uniform, looking tired and stressed.

Voiceover: The emphasis on militarism can also lead to the glorification of war and the dehumanization of soldiers. Soldiers are often seen as heroes, but their experiences of war can lead to physical and mental health issues.

Cut to: A scene where a soldier is struggling with PTSD and seeking help.

Counselor: It's important to remember that there is no shame in seeking help. You've been through a traumatic experience, and it's okay to need support.

Closing Shot: A group of protestors holding signs that say "No to War" and "Invest in Education, Not War."

Voiceover: In order to create a more peaceful world, we need to prioritize education, healthcare, and social welfare, rather than investing in military power.

Closing Message: Let us strive for a world where peace and diplomacy are the first options, rather than militarism and war.

Answer:

Narrator: Militarismo, or militarism, is the belief or desire that a country should maintain a strong military capability and be prepared to use it aggressively to defend or promote national interests. It has been a significant force in shaping global politics and has had both positive and negative effects.

Scene 1: A military parade with soldiers marching, tanks rolling, and planes flying overhead.

Narrator: One of the most visible manifestations of militarism is the military parade. These displays of military might are meant to intimidate enemies and demonstrate a country's power and readiness to defend itself.

Soldier 1: (marching) We are the defenders of our nation. We are ready to fight and die for our country.

Soldier 2: (marching) Our military is the strongest in the world. No one can defeat us.

Scene 2: A group of politicians discussing military spending.

Politician 1: We need to increase military spending to maintain our dominance in the world.

Politician 2: But what about the social programs that are suffering because of the lack of funding?

Politician 1: National security is our top priority. We cannot afford to weaken our military.

Scene 3: A military operation in a foreign country.

Soldier 1: (pointing a gun) We are here to liberate this country from its oppressors.

Soldier 2: (shooting) Our mission is to bring democracy and freedom to this land.

Narrator: Militarism often leads to military interventions in other countries. These interventions can be justified as humanitarian efforts to protect innocent civilians or as necessary actions to defend national interests.

Scene 4: A group of protesters against militarism.

Protester 1: We need to stop pouring our resources into the military-industrial complex.

Protester 2: We should be investing in education, healthcare, and social welfare programs.

Narrator: However, militarism also has its critics. Some argue that it diverts resources away from social programs and perpetuates a culture of violence and aggression.

Scene 5: A soldier returning from war with physical and emotional scars.

Soldier: (limping and crying) War is not glorious. It destroys lives and leaves scars that never heal.

Narrator: The human cost of militarism cannot be ignored. Soldiers and civilians alike suffer physical and emotional trauma from wars and conflicts.

Scene 6: A group of diplomats negotiating a peace treaty.

Diplomat 1: We need to find a way to resolve this conflict without resorting to military action.

Diplomat 2: Agreed. We must use diplomacy and dialogue to find common ground and avoid further bloodshed.

Narrator: Ultimately, the best way to prevent the negative effects of militarism is through diplomacy and peaceful conflict resolution. Only by working together can we create a world free from the threat of war and violence.
