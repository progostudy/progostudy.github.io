Answer: Similarities and differences of Point-and-shoot camera and a DSLR camera.

- One of the biggest differences is in creative control. DSLR cameras allow you to manually control certain aspects of a shot, while most point-and-shoot cameras work best when shooting in automatic mode. A point-and-shoot camera is sometimes called a fixed-lens camera because it cannot swap.

What are the positive and negative aspects of each one?

Point-and-shoot Camera

- Advantages; first, its size - it's portable you can slip it in your pocket, the weight, since it's very light unlike DSLR that is heavy. This camera is cheaper. Disadvantages, quality due to smaller size of the camera sensor, adaptability, limited control, shooting in the dark - the camera does not have a good capabilities for night photography and last one is the inability to capture wide.

DSLR

- Advantages; better image quality, better sensitivity to light, shutter and focus speeds, you see what you shoot, flexible controls, better investment, ability to use different lenses. Disadvantages; very expensive, the weight and size, ongoing maintenance, and the noice.
