14. What is the characteristic of "Je vivroie liement"?


Answer:

Je vivroie liement is a monophonic song by the fourteenth-century French composer Guillaume de Machaut (c.1300-1377). It is an example of the genre known as the virelai, one of the formes fixed of the fourteenth century (the others were the ballade and the rondeau). Schrade catalogues this work as Virelai No. 21.

Answer :Je vivroie liement is a monophonic song by the fourteenth-century French composer Guillaume de Machaut (c.1300-1377). It is an example of the genre known as the virelai, one of the formes fixed of the fourteenth century (the others were the ballade and the rondeau). Schrade catalogues this work as Virelai No. 21.

This is an unusual virelai in form. The usual structure of the virelai is A b b a A (the capital A representing the refrain) repeated thrice. In this virelai, however, this form is repeated twice only. Perhaps because the virelai was a relatively new form, Machaut felt comfortable with structural alteration; certainly his settings of virelais are less structurally uniform than his settings of ballades or rondeaux, despite being relatively conservative in that they are usually monophonic.

30. in the figure at the right JE HN,JE =8cm and two concentric circles have radii of 10 cm and 17 cm​


Answer:

1.) 6 cm

2.) 6 cm

3.) 9 cm

4.) 15 cm

5.) 12 cm

6.) 15 cm

7.) 30 cm

Step-by-step explanation:

1 and 2.

If you look at the circles, you can see two right triangles formed by line JE. We have triangle JEN and triangle JEA. To find the value of EA, we need to use the Pythagorean theorem a² + b² = c².

Let JE be a, EA be b, and JA be c.

JE is equal to 8 as stated in the given. JA is equal to 10 since the radius is 10cm according to the given.

8² + b² = 10²

64 + b² = 100

b² = 36

[tex] \sqrt{ {b }^{2} } = \sqrt{36} [/tex]

b = 6

Therefore, the value of EA is 6 cm. The value of EI is also 6 cm since line JA is perpendicular to line HN, meeting at its midpoint.

3 and 4.

To find the value of IN, we need to find EN first, we will be using the Pythagorean theorem again.

Let JE be a, EN be b, and JN be c.

As it says in the given, the two concentric circles have radii of 10cm and 17cm, so the radius JN's measure would be 17cm. The value of JE is already given, which is 8.

Now we have the values of JE and JN, we need to substitute this in the equation a² + b² = c².

8² + b² = 17²

64 + b² = 289

b² = 225

[tex] \sqrt{b {}^{2} } = \sqrt{225} [/tex]

b = 15

Therefore, the value of EN is 15 cm.

To find the value of IN, we will be subtracting the measure of EI from EN, IN = EN - EI.

IN = 15 - 6

IN = 9

The measurement of IN is 9 cm.

5.

Line AI is equal to the sum of EA and EI.

AI = EA + EI

AI = 6 + 6

AI = 12

Line AI is equal to 12 cm.

6.

The measurement of line EH is equal to line EN. Therefore, line EH is equal to 15 cm.

7.

Line HN is equal to the sum of EH and EN.

HN = EH + EN

HN = 15 + 15

HN = 30

The value of HN is 30 cm.
