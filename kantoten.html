6. what does pyridoxine , pantothenic acid and folic acid essential vitamins have in common ?​


Answer:

Pyridoxine , pantothenic acid and folic acid all belong to the B-group vitamins.

Explanation:

Pyridoxine is also known as vitamin B6. Pantothenic acid is also known as vitamin B5. Folic acid is essentially folate, also known as vitamin B9, and is called as folic acid when incorporated in food supplements.

Vitamins which belong to the B-group are water-soluble and are essential for various metabolic processes. Most of them cannot be stored by the body in prolonged periods. Hence they need to be obtained by consuming foods that contain vitamin B.

Each of the vitamin B-complex has a unique structure and unique function. Vitamins B1, B2, B3, and biotin (B7) aid in aspects of energy production, B6 is for amino acid metabolism, and B12 and folic acid aid in cellular division.

7. Vitamin B2

Vitamin B2 is riboflavin. Milk, meat, eggs, nuts, fortified wheat, and green vegetables are all sources of it, and it can be found in both plant and animal diets.

Many bodily functions are influenced by riboflavin. The skin, digestive tract lining, blood cells, and brain function all depend on it for proper development.

6. Niacin

Vitamin B3 also called Niacin.

It's crucial to have enough niacin, or vitamin B3, in the body for good health in general. Higher levels of niacin can lower cholesterol levels as a treatment.

Vitamin B3 or Niacin has been shown in trials to increase levels of good HDL cholesterol and lower triglycerides as a cholesterol therapy. Niacin also helps to decrease harmful LDL cholesterol in a little way.
