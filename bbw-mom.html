What is commutative of addition?

Commutativity is a property of binary operations where the order of the values does not change the result, that is, a + b = b + a. Another example of commutativity is multiplication, where a × b = b × a.

The operations of subtraction and division are not commutative, since a - b ≠ b - a and a/b ≠ b/a, except for very specific values of a and b.
