5. 12. The guests had been drinking all night. Present Perfect Progressive Past Perfect Progressive Future Perfect Progressive​


Answer:

"The guests had been drinking all night."

Present Perfect Progressive

Past Perfect Progressive

Future Perfect Progressive​

This sentence an example of past perfect progressive. You can tell that this is the correct answer since it uses "had" before the verb in "-ing" form. Present perfect progressive uses "has" or "have", while future perfect progressive usually uses "will have been."

Another clue is that this sentence expresses the duration of time. We know that it is no longer happening. The situation instead took place in the past.

6. The _____tenses describe an action that was in progress but was then finished. They are present perfect progressive , past perfect progressive and future perfect progressive.​


Answer:

The past perfect progressive tenses describe an action that was in progress but was then finished.

Explanation:

SOURCE: grammarly.com/blog/past-perfect-continuous-tense/

Unlike the present perfect continuous, which indicates an action that began in the past and continued up to the present, the past perfect continuous is a verb tense that indicates something that began in the past, continued in the past, and also ended at a defined point in the past.
