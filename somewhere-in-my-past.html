29. what do the following lines suggest; that somewhere a.that somewhere they're nailing a coffin in great haste


Autumn

Answer:

There are some poetic lines that means something more or something different than what it was referring to. Writers can use personification by making objects appeal as a person in his writings or such. And sometimes it is just a means of making words appealing and flowery or beautiful to read and write, by replacing words to somewhat hide the real meaning.

What the line suggests:

People are too eager to keep secrets Some people are too afraid of revealing their sins People are keeping something they don’t want other to find out  

Analyzing the line:

If we consider the introductory part of this answer there are some words that are replaced by other objects to hide its true meaning. Nailing a coffin may mean in a non-literal way is keeping or securing something where no one can reach and where no one can open it and that is the deep ground. Or in a literal way it could mean that somewhere there are people burying the dead. In great haste, meaning they don’t want anyone to know since they are in “haste” or they work fast. So, in conclusion the line might mean that somewhere people are quickly burying some secrets where they don’t want people finding it or knowing it.
