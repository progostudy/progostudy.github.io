When saying or writing tenet, it's a common mistake to add an extra "n" to the end of the word, making it tenent. Given tenet's similarity to the more common word tenant (which means "a resident of a specific place"), this error is quite understandable. So, be careful that your tenets have only one "n!"

Example: The small child, who had yet to form a tenet that forbade stealing, stuffed a doll in her pocket when the toymaker wasn't looking.
