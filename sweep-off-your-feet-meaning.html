25. What meaning of sweeping​


Answer:

The word "sweeping" has multiple meanings depending on the context. Here are a few possible interpretations:

The act of cleaning or tidying up an area by using a broom or brush to move dirt or debris towards a particular location, such as a dustpan or a pile.

The act of moving smoothly and continuously through an area, such as a person or vehicle moving through a curve or a dancer gracefully moving across a stage.

The act of affecting a large area or group of people, such as a sweeping change in policy or a sweeping victory in an election.

The act of conducting a thorough search or inspection of an area or object, such as a security guard sweeping a building for intruders or a scientist sweeping a microscope across a sample to observe its features.

The act of dominating or winning decisively, such as a team sweeping a series of games in a competition or an individual sweeping the awards at a ceremony.
