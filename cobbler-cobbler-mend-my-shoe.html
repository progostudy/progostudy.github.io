23. Give an Analysis about the poor cobbler and the rich man​


The story of the poor cobbler and the rich man is a tale about two men who are very different. The rich man gives the cobbler some money, but the cobbler becomes preoccupied with the money and loses his joy for life. He eventually returns the money to the rich man and becomes happy again. The story highlights the idea that money cannot buy happiness and that material possessions can sometimes be a burden rather than a blessing. The rich man, on the other hand, is portrayed as someone who has everything but is not content with his life. The story suggests that true happiness comes from within and that it is not dependent on external circumstances. In summary, the story of the poor cobbler and the rich man is a cautionary tale about the dangers of materialism and the importance of finding joy in the simple things in life.

Fruit Cobblers

- are an American deep-dish fruit dessert or pie with a thick crust (usually a biscuit crust) and a fruit filling (such as peaches, apples, berries). Some versions are enclosed in the crust, while others have a drop-biscuit or crumb topping.
