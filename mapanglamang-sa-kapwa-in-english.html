1. translate this in english: huwag mapanglamang sa kapwa, matutong magbigay ng tama


The line "huwag mapanglamang sa kapwa, matutong magbigay ng tama" can be translated in English as "Do not be greedy instead learn to give right". We can define greed as an extreme wants and selfish desire for something others have. It often comes from wanting lots of money or material wealth, but in reality, it is not always the case. Any decision to enrich yourself by taking things from others an example of greed and is something that should be avoided at all costs.  Our hunger for getting more power and wealth makes many forget their values.

Real-World Greed

Sadly, in today's world, there are many examples of greed that we find unnoticed and that includes:

A person who takes credit for the hard work of others.Dishonest people who like schemes to steal money from people.A person or company who refuses to pay his income taxes.Banks who give people mortgages more than what the customer can afford.An employee who takes extravagant vacations at the expense of the company not minding the aftereffect to the casual employee.

Overcome Greed

Not everyone is born a natural giver.  The good news is that we can all develop generosity. Here are some ways to overcome greed:

Remember what the Bible tells us; there is more happiness in giving than in receiving.Do not live only for yourself.Always remember that  you cannot bring your wealth  and power when you die.Realize that you could be the answer to someone’s prayers.
