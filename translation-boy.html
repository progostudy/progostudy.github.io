There are 10 more cars than jeep5. A woman's age is 15 years ago 6. The area of rectangle whose lenght is 5 more than the width7. The sum of three consecutive even integers8. The total peso value of 30 coins consisting of 5 coins and 10 coins


Answer:

Translating Mathematical Phrases to Mathematical Expression

A mathematical phrase is a verbal phrase that contains words that can be translated into a mathematical expression.

A mathematical expression is a combination of constant, variables and algebraic operations.

Let us now translate the given phrases above to mathematical expressions.

1. Three less than twice a number

Let x be the number.

2x - 3

2. 5 more than three times a number

Let x be the number.

3x + 5

3. There are twice as many boys as there are girls

Let x be the number of girls; y be the number of boys.

y = 2x

4. There are 10 more cars than jeep

Let x be the number of jeeps; y be the number of cars.

y = x + 10

5. A woman's age is 15 years ago

Let x be the woman's age.

x - 15

6. The area of rectangle whose length is 5 more than the width

Let x be the width; y be the area of rectangle.

y = (5 + x) • x

7. The sum of three consecutive even integers

Let x be the 1st integer.

x + (x + 2) + (x + 4)

8. The total peso value of 30 coins consisting of 5 coins and 10 coins

Let x be the 5 coins; y be the 10 coins; z be the peso value.

z = 5x + 10y
