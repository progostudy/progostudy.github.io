15. Also known as 'kitchen less kitchen'?


Answer:

In the late 1980s, following discussions with clients, legislative bodies and healthcare experts across Canada, Burlodge Canada Ltd. identified the primary challenges healthcare managers were facing in the delivery of dietary services.

• Equipment in many hospital kitchens is outdated and food cannot be stored, prepared, or served in an efficient and sanitary manner. This leads to compromised storage space and temperatures, sub-standard production yields, inefficient staff, and inflexible meal service formats

• High maintenance and repair costs result from the use of older equipment. Many hospital kitchens require renovation and equipment replacement to ensure that equipment malfunction does not impede meal service

• Inappropriate food-serving temperatures due to centralized hot-plating techniques are omnipresent in many institutional dietary services. Moreover, the tray systems often cannot maintain adequate food temperatures from the central plating location to the point of service.

• Inconsistent skill levels of staff has always been an issue. For example, the Shepherd’s Pie made by Chef A on Monday may not taste the same as the Shepherd’s Pie made by Chef B next Monday

• Patient dissatisfaction with services is becoming more prevalent. In addition, old methods of meal delivery and menu design are not keeping up with the expectations of today’s clientele

• Kitchen facilities are underutilized because the original architectural designs called for considerable space allocation and because of shrinking in-patient loads. In today’s kitchens less space is required and in some cases (depending on the amount of food production done on-site) a “kitchen” is not needed at all, thus enabling a hospital to reclaim space for other uses

• The need for self-sustaining cafeterias is becoming more of an issue. Hospitals simply cannot afford to subsidize these operations any longer. In order to be profitable, cafeterias must win back the “brown bag” customer and entice visitors to the hospital.

• Future regionalization and consolidation must be at the forefront when developing new approaches to meal preparation and service. The challenge is to reengineer today while reducing potential redundancy for tomorrow.

• Reduced allocation of public funds brings with it caution about capital projects. Often there is a need to forgo kitchen renovation projects for other “front of the house” needs or medical equipment. Solutions that push the envelope and consider a “kitchenless” orientation are becoming more prevalent today.

19. difference between kitchen tools,kitchen utensils and kitchen equipment


Kitchen Tools, Utensils, and Equipment: Its Difference

Kitchen Tools

Kitchen tools refers to the tools in kitchen which are handheld. Anything or tools you can hold with a hand are kitchen tools.

Kitchen Utensils

Kitchen utensils are handheld, hence, it can be considered as kitchen tools. These kitchen utensils are tools you used to cook with.

Examples of kitchen utensils

Knife or knives - these are bladed or sharp tools used to chop or mince foods.

Measuring cup or spoons - this is used to hold specific amount of dry and wet ingredients.

Strainers- used to separate liquid from solid

Pot- holds the food we cook or where the food are put to cook

Pan- used specifically for frying

Can or bottle openner- used to open can and bottle

Chopping board- a hard tool where food can be chopped or minced

Kitchen Equipment

Kitchen equipment are the things used in the kitchen which needs electricity. These are bigger compared to kitchen utensils.

Examples of kitchen equipment

Refrigerator- this is used to prolong the freshness of the foods

Oven- this thermally insulated chamber used for baking

Stove- used for cooking process. Dependent to direct heat

Range- a set or combination of a stove and oven in one equipment

Blender- used to mix, grind, stir and grate foods

24. •cookery. kitchen tools and kitchen Equipment​


Answer:

1. Chef’s Knife

A pairing knife, a serrated knife, and a chef’s knife – these are three knives that every kitchen needs. For mincing and slicing smaller items you will need a paring knife, for cutting bread you need to use a serrated knife

2. Kitchen Shears

If you want to cut meat and fish into small pieces then kitchen shears are really handy. This tool serves multiple purposes and when you will find the usefulness, you will be amazed.

3. Measuring Cups and Spoons

Another essential kitchen tool is measuring cups and spoons. They are very common nowadays. Everyone is very concerned about our health. And rather than an approximation, these items help you to measure the exact calculation of the ingredients.

4. Vegetable Peeler

For cutting the outer skin of vegetables, it is a necessary tool. A vegetable peeler has a narrow blade and a long and easy to grip handle.

5. Spatula and Tongs

During cooking for tossing and flipping items a turner tool is necessary. With a spatula, you can easily flip patties, pancakes, or eggs. There are different types of spatula are available on the market such as rubber spatula, metal spatula, offset spatula, etc.

Another essential kitchen tool is tongs. It works great when you pan-frying chicken as it can keep your hands at arm’s length, also, it is great for flipping steaks, and agitating pasta.

6. Mixing Bowls

For the pre-cooking session, a mixing bowl is essential. Before turning the stoves on, in the right order often have to mix up ingredients. For every cook, it is a mandatory item.

7. Cutting Boards

To cut your items accurately and safely, you’ll need a base. And for this purpose, a custom cutting board is the perfect tool. There are various sizes of cutting boards available and you need multiple boards for the different items that you’ll be cutting. A bamboo cutting board is better than other cutting boards. This is because it can help to protect you from viral diseases as bamboo is naturally antibacterial. On the other hand, bacterial or viral diseases often spread easily when using a plastic cutting board, especially if you do not clean the board properly.

8. Skillet

In your skillet, you can cook a wide range of food. For tossing and frying the item it is an essential tool. In a frying pan, you will do most of your cooking. There are stainless steel and nonstick pans you will find in the market, so it is better to buy several. For pancakes or eggs (for the light food item) a very handy cooking tool is a nonstick pan.

9. Colander

An essential task before cooking is washing the food. And from the boiled food, meat, or vegetables to strain excess water and rinse a colander will help you. Also, it can work as a salad spinner if you like to eat salad regularly.

10. Box Grater

For many people, a box grater is an essential item that should be in the kitchen. You can slice or shred cheese, fruits, or vegetables in it.

11. WhisK

You may often need to mix up different ingredients in cooking. And for this task, a very convenient tool is a whisk. To blend different types of components smoothly, the wires of the whisk are evenly distributed. Choose a whisk with a narrow handle that is mid-sized. This can blend easily and grip precisely.

12. Baking Sheet

If you are a cake lover then you won’t miss this item in your kitchen. In this pan, you can prepare light dinner, food and of course bake cakes. Also, you can serve food in it and it is easy to clean.

13. Saucepan

The saucepan can distribute heat well and it is amusing to cook with it. In a saucepan, you can boil eggs, you can cook pasta, soup, or rice. It is safe to use as it comes with a stainless steel handle.

14. Dutch Oven

For slow cooking, a perfect tool is a dutch oven that is made of cast iron. With little evaporation for hours, it provides the slow transfer of heat. And you can preserve your food in a dutch oven. Additionally, when you have multiple items to cook, this will complement your saucepan.

15. Pyrex Storage Set

Without those boxes, you can’t imagine a kitchen. When it comes to keeping your food they are a lifesaver. You can keep them in your oven, microwave, or refrigerator. You can also carry food in your office, and you can clean them easily.

16. Instant Pot

Like a slow cooker, rice cooker, or pressure cooker you can use this kitchen item. You can also steam vegetables in it, and for this, you need to place a vegetable steamer in it.

17. Blender

One of the most useful kitchen items is a blender. If you love to drink smoothie, juice, milkshake, protein shake, or cocktail then a blender is a must for you. Besides, to enjoy tasty drinks you will need the best blender for smoothies or for other kinds of drinks.
