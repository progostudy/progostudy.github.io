14. if Xin is five years older than Xia and the product of their ages is 150 what are their present ages?


Answer:

15 and 10

Step-by-step explanation:

15 x 10 = 150

n (n+5) = 150

n²+5n= 150

Equate to zero

n²+5n-150= 0

Find its factor

(n+15) (n-10)

Equate to zero to find its root

n+15= 0 n-10= 0

n= - 15 n= 10

Disregard its sign as we don't have a negative age.

So the ages are 15 and 10

20. Explain each concept of five constant and provide examples REN YI LI ZHI XINano sagot?​


Answer:

Ren: (Chinese: 人), “humanity,” “humaneness,” “goodness,” “benevolence,” or “love” Wade-Giles romanization jen, the foundational virtue of Confucianism. It characterizes the bearing and behaviour that a paradigmatic human being exhibits in order to promote a flourishing human community.

Yi, (Chinese: 義), “justice, righteousness; meaning,” is an important concept in Confucianism. It involves a moral disposition to do good, and also the intuition and sensibility to do so competently.

Li, (Chinese: 李 ), Confucian concept often rendered as “ritual,” “proper conduct,” or “propriety.” Originally li denoted court rites performed to sustain social and cosmic order. ... From customary patterns, li came to mean conventional norms, yielding a new concept of an internalized code, ( of civility that defined proper human conduct.

Zhi, (Chinese: 至 ), another Confucian virtue that means knowledge. Knowledge is the base for wisdom. Consequently, only knowledgeable and wise people can use Confucian moral theory benefitting others. Wisdom is the innate knowledge of every person and his/her ability to implement it in practice.

Xin, (Chinese: 心), refers to the physical heart, though it also refers to the "mind" as the ancient Chinese believed the heart was the center of human cognition. For these reasons, it is also often translated as "heart-mind". It has a connotation of intention, yet can be used to refer to long-term goals.
