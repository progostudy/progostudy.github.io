2. Ligaya Victorio Fruto's brief history and how did her works influenced people


Ligaya Victorious Fruto was born and raised in Philippines. She was a trained teacher on Philippine Normal School and lately she taught in Baguio. When she was a teens she began to write and publish stories. And her works won acclaim  for her short stories in Honolulu, Hawaii. Fruto was a journalist of pre war Tribute, forerunner of the Manila. But during Japanese occupation she was pick up of Japanese soldiers for writing wartime editorials. She married a newspaper reporter Ramon Reyes but unfortunately he died on World War II. In 1946 she join the press office of the Philippines President Manuel Roxas and lately she married Honolulu Engineer Lorenzo Fruto and lived in Hawaii. She wrote two books the Yesterday and Other Stories and One Rainbow for the Duration.

24. Summarise of lockdown victories


Answer:

Space is something we Filipinos don't care too much about. To name a few examples: our houses our built right next to each other; there is minimal to non-existent personal space; shoppers are literally rubbing elbows at the biggest public market in the city; and passengers are squished inside a small jeepney.

But space is something our founders thought an important factor when building a child-caring center. During this pandemic time, where children are prohibited to go out of their homes, our children are still able to "go out" of their homes. They are still able to enjoy a game of soccer and kickball in our huge playground. Our toddlers are still able to ride their scooters on our driveways. Our children are still able to run around and chase each other in a game of tag. The space we have makes the lockdown endurable for everyone.
