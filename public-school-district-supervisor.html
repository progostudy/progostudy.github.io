21. Voters in a district who elect a public servant are known as a(n) ?


Answer:An individual voter in a district is called a Constituent. As a group, they are called a Constituency.They are also called an Electorate.

Explanation:

A Constituent is A VOTING MEMBER of a community or organization that has the power to appoint or elect a candidate into a position. They also have the power to remove an elected representative from a position and are called an Electorate.

The Electorates are the people or citizens or constituencies who are registered to vote their representatives in a democracy. They are commonly called Voters.

A Constituency is ALL of the constituents of an appointed representative.
