Answer:

Filipino people frequently experience abuse and social exclusion, which stems from discrimination. And as discrimination is prevalent in our civilization, many of our compatriots often encounter other people, whether foreign or local, with a general dislike and hatred for indigenous tribes, people with disabilities (PWDs), individuals with HIV/AIDS, and other socially secluded sectors of the society. Our fellow Filipinos constantly face cruel remarks and prejudiced slurs from locals every day simply because one might belong to a low-class family or a different type of religion. OFWs abroad also suffer bigotry and inequity concerning race, caste, ethnicity, and nationality.

People seem to forget that our fellows are also children of God who deserve the same kind of comforts and treatment that we received. Others are too blinded by discrimination to acknowledge their flaws and treat others with more respect and kindness. As the saying goes, "Do not do unto others what you don't want others to do unto you." Therefore, we must offer other people the interchangeable pleasure of living in a discrimination-free world.
