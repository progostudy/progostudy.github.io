Answer:

In Lesson 1, Chapter 3, pages 84 to 88, the focus is on the aural realm, specifically the concept of sound, silence, music, and noise. The chapter highlights that sound is created by vibrations that travel through a medium, while silence is the absence of sound. Music is defined as organized sound, while noise refers to unwanted or unpleasant sound.

The chapter also explores the different ways sound can be categorized, such as pitch, volume, and timbre. Pitch is the highness or lowness of a sound, while volume refers to the loudness or softness of a sound. Timbre is the quality of sound that distinguishes different instruments or voices.

The chapter goes on to discuss the cultural and social significance of sound, such as how it can be used to communicate, express emotions, or evoke specific reactions. It also touches on the role of sound in media and technology, such as in film, television, and music production.

Overall, the chapter provides an overview of the various components of the aural realm and their significance in different contexts, highlighting the importance of sound and its impact on our daily lives.

29. Explain this. Go placiddy amid the noise and hoste and remember what peace there may be in silence​


Answer:

"Go placidly amid the noise and haste and remember what peace there may be in silence" is a line from the poem "Desiderata" by Max Ehrmann. The line encourages the reader to remain calm and peaceful in the midst of a busy and noisy world.

In our fast-paced and often chaotic world, it can be easy to get caught up in the noise and hustle of everyday life. This line reminds us to take a step back, to find moments of stillness and silence, and to remember that there is peace to be found in the midst of it all.

By encouraging us to "go placidly," the line suggests that we should move through life calmly and without unnecessary haste. We should not let the noise and chaos of the world around us dictate our actions or our emotions. Instead, we should seek out moments of stillness and calm, and find peace in the silence.

Overall, this line reminds us of the importance of finding balance in our lives, and taking the time to appreciate the moments of peace and tranquility that can be found even in the midst of a busy and noisy world.
