The Sui dynasty ([swěi], Chinese: 隋朝; pinyin: Suí cháo) was a short-lived imperial dynasty of China of pivotal significance. The Sui unified the Northern and Southern dynasties and reinstalled the rule of ethnic Han in the entirety of China proper, along with sinicization of former nomadic ethnic minorities (Five Barbarians) within its territory. 22. INTEGUMENTARY SYSTEMsou​


The Integumentary System

Functions of the integumentary Sytem

The human skin has several functions:

It protects the body's internal living tissues and organs.

It serves as on of the body's first lines of defense against pathogenic and infectious organisms.

It protects the body from dehydration and against abrupt changes in temperature.

It acts as receptor for torch, pressure, pain, and temperature.

It stores water and fat.

It helps in the production of vitamin D.

Temperature Regulation

Humans are considered as endothermic or warm-blooded organisms, hence, we are able to regulate our body temperature. We can perform thermoregulation through perspiration in our body. As sweat evaporates, it absorbs body heat, thereby cooling the body. When the temperature is low, goosebumps may occur. Goosebumps result in the erection of epidermal hairs due to low temperature. However an extremely low temperature can cause a certain condition which is referred to as the hypothermia Hypothermia is a medical condition in which the temperature of your body is very low. This condition, if left untreated, can eventually lead to organ failure, specifically the heart and the organs of the respiratory system, and eventually to death.

Protection and Senses

The skin serves as one of the first line of defenses and gives us protection from germs and foreign substances. The skin prevents them from entering our body thus preventing them from causing us diseases. In addition to protection against pathogens and foreign bodies, the skin also helps maintain body temperature by preventing excessive water loss. We are also protected from ultraviolet radiation. The melanin in our skin gives our skin its color and protects us from the harmful ultraviolet rays of the sun. People who live in areas in the world that are annually exposed to the sun's radiation, such as areas near the equator, tend to have darker complexion as a means for them to be more protected from the sun's harmful ultraviolet radiation. Our skin is also equipped with receptors that detect information such as pain, heat, pressure, and temperature changes. The nerves in our skin receive the information which are then transmitted to the brain.

Structure of the Skin

The skin is an organ of the integumentary system that covers the entire body surface. Moreover, the integumentary system is also composed of the hair and nails. Our skin is composed of four types of tissues, namely: epithelial tissues, connective tissues, muscle tissues, and nerve tissues. These tissues work together to allow the skin to perform its specific functions. The epithelial tissues cover the body surface, while connective tissues such as the tendons and ligaments provide support and allow us to perform locomotive and other related activities. Lastly, muscle tissues are involved in body movement, and nerve tissues form body communication network. Through the coordination of these tssues, we are able to perform specific biological activities. The skin is also composed of different layers that have specific structures and functions.

28. Ilarawan ang Sui dynasty?


Ang Sui dynasty ay isang dinastiya sa China. Ito ay isang “legalist” at militaristikong dinastiya. Ito ay nagumpisa noong 581 mula 618 AD at ito ang isa mga maikling dinastiya sa China. Kahit maiksi ang panahon ng Sui dynasty ay nagawa nito ang “Grand Canal” sa China. Sumunod sa Sui Dynasty ang Tang Dynasty.
