23. How i felt being High School Student?​


My high school experience was very different from what I had in middle school. I had more options on campus to get active and a larger selection of classes to pick from.

When I first started high school, I was both frightened and eager. Freshmen orientation was a surprising event; it seemed like something out of a high school musical. Over the course of my first year, I found my experience to be somewhat mundane. There was little need to be concerned, but I was since I had no idea what to anticipate.

Make a solid first impression, focus your studies, and participate in as many extracurricular activities as your school affords, because "how you felt" will matter far less.

29. role in life that how I felt about it


You're quite too young to know what your role in life is. Normally, that's something you'll realize once you hit your 20s, probably beyond your college years. But if you're already thinking about it at this point, that's okay. 

To help you with this adult-y question, I suggest you start with your current role, maybe in school, or in the family. Are you a club president, or are you the eldest among your siblings? Then from there, you can let people know how you feel about it. You can either be:

1. Happy that you're able to help the family by simply providing moral support since you're still in school

2. Burdened with a responsibility

3. Challenged, since being responsible is more of an adult thing and you're not quite ready for that

These are just examples, so feel free to come up with your own. The most important thing is that you draw from your own experience so it would be easy for you to know what to write.
