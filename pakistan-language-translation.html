10. research of pakistan


Answer:

Pakistan is a country located in South Asia, bordered by India to the east, Afghanistan and Iran to the west, China to the northeast, and the Arabian Sea to the south. The country has a rich history dating back thousands of years and has undergone significant political, social, and economic changes in recent decades.

Geography and Climate:

Pakistan is a country with diverse geography, including the Himalayan and Karakoram mountain ranges in the north, the Indus River plain, and the Arabian Sea coastline. The country experiences a range of climatic conditions, from the hot and dry desert climate in the south to the cool and temperate climate in the northern mountainous regions.

Population and Demographics:

Pakistan has a population of approximately 225 million people, making it the fifth-most populous country in the world. The population is predominantly Muslim, with other religious minorities such as Hindus, Christians, and Sikhs also present.

Economy:

Pakistan's economy is largely dependent on agriculture, which employs a significant portion of the population. The country also has significant mineral and energy resources, including coal, natural gas, and oil. Pakistan is a member of the World Trade Organization and has recently implemented economic reforms to attract foreign investment and improve its business climate.

Politics:

Pakistan has a federal parliamentary democratic republic system of government, with the President as the head of state and the Prime Minister as the head of government. The country has a history of military rule and political instability, but has seen significant improvements in recent years with a peaceful transfer of power between civilian governments in 2013 and 2018.

Culture and Society:

Pakistan has a rich cultural heritage, with a blend of South Asian, Central Asian, and Middle Eastern influences. The country has a diverse array of languages, with Urdu and English being the official languages. Pakistani cuisine is known for its variety of spices and flavors, with dishes such as biryani, kebabs, and curries being popular.

Challenges and Opportunities:

Pakistan faces a number of challenges, including poverty, inequality, terrorism, and sectarian violence. The country also faces significant environmental challenges, including deforestation, water scarcity, and air pollution. However, Pakistan has significant opportunities for growth and development, including its strategic location as a hub for trade and commerce between Asia and the Middle East, and its large, young, and growing population.

In conclusion, Pakistan is a country with a rich history, diverse culture, and significant challenges and opportunities. While the country faces significant political, social, and economic challenges, there is potential for growth and development in the coming years.

Answer:

Pakistan and India share a rich cultural heritage, and their music is no exception. While there are similarities in the vocal and instrumental music of these two neighboring countries, there are also distinct differences.

In terms of vocal music, both Pakistan and India have a long tradition of classical music that is based on ragas and talas. However, there are some differences in the styles of singing. For example, Pakistani classical vocal music is known for its subtle and refined style, while Indian classical vocal music often features more ornamentation and improvisation. In addition, the use of the ghazal form is more common in Pakistani music, while Indian music features a greater emphasis on bhajans and kirtans, which are devotional songs.

When it comes to instrumental music, both countries have a variety of traditional instruments, such as the sitar, tabla, sarangi, and flute. However, Pakistani instrumental music is known for its use of the harmonium, which is a type of keyboard instrument, as well as the sarod, which is a stringed instrument that is similar to the sitar but has a different sound. Indian instrumental music, on the other hand, is known for its use of the veena, which is a large, multi-stringed instrument that is similar to the sitar, but with a different playing style.

Overall, while there are some similarities in the vocal and instrumental music of Pakistan and India, there are also distinct differences that reflect the unique cultural traditions and influences of each country.
