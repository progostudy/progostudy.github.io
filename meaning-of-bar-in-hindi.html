4. bar chart meaning and explanation​


Explanation:

A bar chart, also known as a bar graph, is a graphical representation of data using rectangular bars. The length or height of each bar represents the magnitude of the data being shown. The bars can be vertically or horizontally arranged and are usually drawn adjacent to each other to allow for comparison between categories or values. Bar charts are often used to display categorical data and help to visually compare different groups or categories of data, to highlight trends or patterns, and to communicate information in a clear and concise manner.
