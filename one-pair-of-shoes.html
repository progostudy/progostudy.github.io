1. A man has 10 shirts, 8 pairs of pants and three pairs of shoes. How many different outfits, consisting of one shirt, one pair of pants and one pair of shoes, are possible? ​


Answer:

240

Step-by-step explanation:

For each shirt 8 pairs of pants can be worn. That makes 80 different outfits, 8 x 10, with a different combination of a shirt and pants. Now for each shirt and pants combination 3 pairs of shoes can be worn. That would make a total of 80 x 3 equal to 240 different combinations of outfits.

10 shirts times 8 pairs of pants times 3 pairs of shoes equals the total combinations.

10 x 8 x 3 = 240

therefore the answer to your question is 240 different combinations are possible. QED

9. a shoe factory can finish 5 pairs of ladies shoes for every 2 pairs of mens shoes. if it finishes 60 pairs of ladies shoes hkw many pairs of mens shoes can it finish?​


Answer: The factory can finish 24 pairs of men's shoes.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let x be the number of pairs of men's shoes that the factory can finish.

The factory can finish 5 pairs of ladies shoes for every 2 pairs of men's shoes, so we can set up the following proportion:

5/2 = 60/x

Cross-multiplying, we get:

5x = 2 * 60

Simplifying, we get:

5x = 120

Dividing both sides by 5, we get:

x = 24

Therefore, the factory can finish 24 pairs of men's shoes.

16. Is putting on a pair of socks and shoes on ones feet is commutative or non commutative​


Answer:

Non commutative

Step-by-step explanation:

They are commutative if you find that putting your shoes on first and then your socks gives the same result as putting your socks on first and then the shoes. If it is not the same, then the activities are non commutative.

Answer:

Answer is noncommutative.

Step-by-step explanation.
