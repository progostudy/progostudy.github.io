13. Resolution of The Happiest Boy In The World​


Resolution of The Happiest Boy in the World

The Happiest Boy in the World is a story composed by the late Filipino writer, N.V.M Gonzales. In the story, the Resolution of The Happiest Boy in the World is when Jose, the 12 year-old son of Julio, finished reading his father's letter and finally began to understand what it means. After realizing what the letter meant, his emotion is filled with curiosity and joy. As the story further concluded, he and his father parted ways as soon as he read the letter. With that, even the world seems to be rejoicing with Jose.

27. Exposition of the happiest boy in the world


Exposition is the plot of the story where characters, settings, and conflict are introduced.

In the story The Happiest Boy In The World by N.V.M. Gonzales, the exposition is found in the first paragraph:

Julio, who had come from Tablas to settle in Barok, was writing a letter to Ka Ponso, his landlord, one warm June night. It was about his son, Jose, who wanted to go to school in Mansalay that year. Julio had some difficulty in getting some land of his own to farm, the boy had to stop schooling for a year. As it was, Julio thought himself lucky enough to have Ka Ponso take him on as tenant. Later, when Julio's wife Fidela gave birth to a baby, Ka Ponso offered to become its godfather. After that they began to call each other compadre.

Characters introduced where: Jose, Julio (father of Jose), Fidela (mother of Jose), Ka Ponso (landlord)

Setting: Barok; one warm June night

Conflict: Jose wants to go to school in Mansalay, but his father had difficulty in getting a land so Jose have to stop schooling
