26. One of the most common languages spokenin Africa is Swahili​


Answer:

The most spoken language in Africa is Swahili which is said to have between 100 and 150 million speakers. Known as a ‘Bantu’ language, Swahili apparently originated from other languages like Arabic. This is the official language of Tanzania, Uganda, the Democratic Republic of Congo and Kenya, but it is also used in countries like Ethiopia, Sudan, Burundi, Rwanda, southern Somalia, northern Mozambique and the Comoros Islands. Swahili is the main medium of instruction in schools and it’s not considered a difficult language to learn, especially if you already know some Arabic.

Fun Fact? Swahili was the language used in The Lion King. Remember ‘Hakuna Matata’? That means no worries in Swahili and ‘Simba’ means ‘lion’!


27. What advice would you give to calm down?​


Answer:

To calm down, take deep breaths, practice mindfulness, engage in physical activity, take a break, practice relaxation techniques, and talk to someone for support and guidance.

Answer:

but here are some tips that may help you calm down:

1. Take deep breaths: Slow and deep breaths can help reduce stress, anxiety and calm your mind. Focus on your breath and try to relax.

2. Take a break: If you feel overwhelmed, take a break and step away from the situation. Give yourself time to calm down and come back to it when you feel ready.

3. Engage in physical activities: Exercise is a great way to relieve stress and tension. Go for a walk, run, or do some yoga to release the pent-up energy.

4. Listen to calming music: Music can be a powerful tool to calm down. Find some soothing music that you enjoy and listen to it.

5. Practice mindfulness: Mindfulness involves focusing on the present moment and accepting things as they are. Practicing mindfulness can help you stay calm and centered, even in stressful situations.

6. Seek support: Talk to someone you trust about what's bothering you. It can be a friend, family member, or a professional. Speaking to someone can help you gain perspective and feel better.
