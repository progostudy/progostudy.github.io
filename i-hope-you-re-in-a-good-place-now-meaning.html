Explanation:

In a school setting, a student might know that studying and doing homework are important for their academic success, but they find themselves procrastinating or getting distracted by other activities. Similarly, a student might know that it is wrong to cheat on a test, but they succumb to the pressure to do so.

In a home setting, a person might know that it is important to be kind and patient with their family members, but they find themselves getting frustrated and lashing out in anger or saying hurtful things.

In an environmental context, a person might know that it is important to reduce their carbon footprint and take steps to protect the environment, but they find themselves consistently choosing convenience over sustainability or not taking action due to a lack of perceived impact.
