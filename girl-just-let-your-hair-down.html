5. Suppose that 5/8 of the students in a class are girls, and 3/7 of the girls have long hair. If there are 35 girls in the class, how many of them have long hair?


Answer:

15 girls in the class have long hair.

Step-by-step explanation:

First, we need to find out the total number of students in the class.

Let x be the total number of students in the class. Then, we can write an equation based on the given information:

5/8x = number of girls in the class

3/7(5/8x) = number of girls with long hair

We know that there are 35 girls in the class, so we can set these two expressions equal to each other and solve for x:

5/8x = 35

x = (35)(8/5)

x = 56

So there are 56 students in the class.

Now we can use the second expression to find out how many of the 35 girls have long hair:

3/7(5/8(56)) = 15

Therefore, 15 girls in the class have long hair.

16. why do our hairs get white when we're getting old? just curiosity:)​


Answer:

As we get older, the pigment cells in our hair follicles gradually die. When there are fewer pigment cells in a hair follicle, that strand of hair will no longer contain as much melanin and will become a more transparent color — like gray, silver, or white — as it grows. ... People can get gray hair at any age.
