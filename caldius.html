3. Calcium carbonate (CaCO3), also called calcite. a. Calculate the molar mass of calcium carbonate. b. A certain sample of calcium carbonate contains 4.86 moles. What is the mass in grams of this sample? What is the mass of the CO3-2 ions present?


Answer:

a. To calculate the molar mass of calcium carbonate, we need to determine the number of grams per mole for each element present in the compound. The molar mass of calcium carbonate can be calculated using the following formula:

Molar mass (g/mol) = (Mass of Ca (g/mol)) + (Mass of C (g/mol)) + (Mass of O (g/mol))

The atomic weights of Ca, C, and O are 40.08 g/mol, 12.01 g/mol, and 16.00 g/mol, respectively. Using these values, the molar mass of calcium carbonate can be calculated as follows:

Molar mass of CaCO3 = (40.08 g/mol) + (12.01 g/mol) + (3 * 16.00 g/mol)

= 40.08 g/mol + 12.01 g/mol + 48.00 g/mol

= 100.09 g/mol

Thus, the molar mass of calcium carbonate is 100.09 g/mol.

b. If a certain sample of calcium carbonate contains 4.86 moles, the mass in grams of this sample can be calculated using the molar mass of calcium carbonate:

Mass (g) = (Number of moles) * (Molar mass)

= (4.86 moles) * (100.09 g/mol)

= 486.0 g

The mass of the CO3-2 ions present in this sample can be calculated using the molar mass of CO3-2, which is 60.01 g/mol:

Mass of CO3-2 ions = (Number of moles of CO3-2) * (Molar mass of CO3-2)

= (4.86 moles) * (60.01 g/mol)

= 291.6 g

Thus, the mass of the CO3-2 ions present in the sample of calcium carbonate is 291.6 g.

10. Difference between a calcium atom and a calcium ion


The difference between a calcium atom and a calcium ion is the number of electrons

Electron atom Ca = 20 while calcium ion (Ca²⁺)= 18

Further explanation

The Atomic Number (Z) indicates the number of protons in an atom of an element. If the atom is neutral then the number of protons will be equal to the number of electrons. So the atomic number can also indicate the number of electrons.

 So atomic number = number of protons = number of electrons

Mass Number (A) is the sum of protons and neutrons

Mass Number (A) = Number of protons + Number of Neutrons

So that the relationship between atomic numbers and mass numbers can be formulated as follows:

Atomic Number (Z) = Mass Number (A) - Number of Neutrons
